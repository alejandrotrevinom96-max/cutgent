# Sources

## Canonical (core method)
- Karen Berman & Joe Knight — *Financial Intelligence (for Entrepreneurs)*. Reading
  the three statements, the difference between profit and cash, key ratios for non-
  financial managers.
- Mike Michalowicz — *Profit First*. Cash-management behavior: allocate revenue into
  separate accounts (profit, owner pay, tax, operating) on receipt.
- Greg Crabtree — *Simple Numbers, Straight Talk, Big Profits*. Owner labor as a
  real cost, contribution and labor-efficiency thinking for small business.
- Bill Aulet — *Disciplined Entrepreneurship* (unit economics: LTV, COCA/CAC).
- Brad Feld & Jason Mendelson — *Venture Deals*. Funding mechanics, debt vs equity,
  dilution, use-of-funds (for the funding-basics boundary only).

## Reference / standards
- U.S. Small Business Administration (SBA) — guidance on cash-flow, pricing,
  bookkeeping, and funding for small businesses.
- IRS Small Business and Self-Employed resources — cash vs accrual basis,
  recordkeeping, estimated taxes (U.S.-specific; confirm with a CPA).
- Accounting fundamentals: the accounting equation (Assets = Liabilities + Equity),
  GAAP/IFRS basics, and standard statement structure (any introductory accounting
  text).

## Note on freshness and jurisdiction
Tax rates, thresholds, deductibility, filing rules, entity treatment, and funding
norms change over time and differ by country/state and by entity type — treat any
specific figure here as point-in-time and re-verify with a current CPA/accountant
before acting. This pack is general education, not accounting, tax, legal, or
investment advice. The `personal/` layer (the user's own numbers, entity,
jurisdiction, and prior professional advice) supersedes everything here when present.
