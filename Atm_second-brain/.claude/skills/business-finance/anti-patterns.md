# Business-Finance Anti-Patterns (detectable failure modes + fix)

## 1. Confusing revenue with profit with cash
**Tell:** "We did $1M in sales" used as proof of health; spending against billed-
but-uncollected revenue; assuming a profitable month means money in the bank.
**Fix:** track all three separately. Revenue is billed, profit is revenue minus
expenses, cash is what's actually in the account. Decisions are paid for in cash.

## 2. No runway view
**Tell:** can't state cash on hand, monthly net burn, or how many months are left.
**Fix:** compute runway = cash ÷ net burn now, recompute monthly, and act when it
drops under ~6 months. A 13-week cash forecast surfaces the failure date early.

## 3. Mixing personal and business money
**Tell:** one card for groceries and inventory; "I'll sort it at tax time."
**Fix:** separate accounts and cards immediately. Commingling corrupts the books,
weakens liability protection, and creates tax and audit risk.

## 4. Pricing below true cost (cost-plus blindness)
**Tell:** margin is figured on materials only; labor, payment fees, returns, support,
and overhead are ignored; price is "cost times a round number."
**Fix:** fully load the cost, confirm positive contribution margin, then price to
value and a target margin — not naive cost-plus.

## 5. Ignoring taxes until they're due
**Tell:** gross receipts treated as spendable income; no reserve; surprise bill.
**Fix:** sweep a tax set-aside into a separate account as you earn. Confirm the rate
and obligations (income, self-employment, sales/VAT, payroll) with a CPA — never
assert a rate as certain.

## 6. Vanity-metric focus
**Tell:** celebrating downloads, signups, GMV, followers, or top-line growth while
contribution margin, cash, and retention go unmentioned.
**Fix:** anchor on metrics tied to cash and margin — contribution margin, net burn,
LTV/CAC, runway, retention. Ask of any metric: does moving it produce cash?

## 7. Scaling on negative unit economics
**Tell:** raising ad spend / headcount / volume while losing money per unit "to grow
into profitability."
**Fix:** make contribution margin positive first. Until then, growth multiplies
losses and shortens runway.

## 8. Unreconciled / stale books and "raise for cushion" funding
**Tell:** books months behind, never reconciled; or raising money with no specific
use-of-funds beyond "extend runway / have a buffer."
**Fix:** reconcile every account monthly so reports are trustworthy; tie any raise to
a concrete milestone and use-of-funds, weighing debt vs equity vs bootstrapping
(dilution, control, repayment risk).
