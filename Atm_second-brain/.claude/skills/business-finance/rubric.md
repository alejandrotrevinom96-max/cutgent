# Business-Finance Readiness Rubric (binary — every box must hold before advising)

- [ ] Runway is computed as cash on hand ÷ net monthly burn, with the actual numbers stated (or flagged as unknown).
- [ ] Net burn is derived from cash out minus cash in, not from the P&L profit line.
- [ ] Revenue, profit, and cash are kept distinct (no claim that being profitable means having cash, or vice versa).
- [ ] Business and personal accounts/cards are confirmed separate; commingling is flagged if present.
- [ ] The right statement is used for the question (P&L for profitability, balance sheet for position, cash-flow for cash movement).
- [ ] Contribution margin (price − variable cost) is computed and confirmed positive before recommending growth/spend.
- [ ] CAC and LTV are defined with their inputs, and LTV/CAC plus CAC payback are checked against a stated heuristic (not asserted as law).
- [ ] Breakeven (fixed costs ÷ contribution margin per unit) is calculated where pricing/volume decisions are made.
- [ ] Pricing is set against value and a target margin using fully-loaded cost — not naive cost-plus — and true cost includes labor/fees/returns/support.
- [ ] Fixed vs variable costs are distinguished, and cash vs accrual basis is identified (basis choice flagged to a CPA).
- [ ] A bookkeeping cadence is named: categorize regularly, reconcile every account monthly against statements.
- [ ] Budget-vs-actual and a rolling 12-month (or 13-week) cash forecast are in place or recommended.
- [ ] A tax set-aside in a separate account is recommended; no tax rate or treatment is asserted as certain.
- [ ] Any funding suggestion is tied to a concrete use-of-funds and milestone, with the debt/equity/bootstrap tradeoff named.
- [ ] CPA/accountant escalation is explicitly named for filings, tax treatment, basis, and entity structuring.
- [ ] `personal/` recall has been consulted; any conflict with generic advice is flagged and personal/ wins.
