# Brand Identity Exemplars (borrow the mechanism, not the surface)

## 1. Mailchimp (R/GA + in-house) — personality as the system, not the logo
The strategy ("empower the underdog small business; be the helpful weird friend")
drives everything: Cavendish-yellow ownable color, the Freddie wink mark, hand-drawn
illustration, and a famously specific *voice & tone* doc (jokes allowed in success
states, never in errors). **Mechanism:** a sharp personality + an explicit tone-range
guide make the brand recognizable even in plain text. **Borrow:** write tone rules
per emotional context (error vs win vs apology), not one "friendly" setting.

## 2. Airbnb "Bélo" (DesignStudio) — one symbol carrying positioning
The mark unifies people + places + love + an "A" into a single drawable symbol,
tied to the strategy of "Belong Anywhere." It was built as a system (Cereal type
family, photography rules, motion) so anyone could extend it. **Mechanism:** a
symbol that *encodes the brand idea* and is simple enough to draw by hand travels
and scales. **Borrow:** compress the positioning into one ownable, reproducible
shape; ship the system (type, photo, motion) around it, not just the glyph.

## 3. Stripe — restraint + an ownable device
Clean wordmark, a disciplined color logic, and the ownable diagonal-gradient
"prism/threads" device that signals premium developer infrastructure. The visual
restraint *is* the positioning (serious, precise, modern). **Mechanism:** a single
distinctive device + ruthless consistency reads as confidence and quality.
**Borrow:** pick one ownable graphic device and apply it everywhere instead of
decorating each surface differently.

## 4. NASA "Worm" (Danne & Blackburn, 1975) — the monochrome/scalability test
A single-weight custom wordmark with no enclosure, designed to reproduce in any
medium at any size (vehicles, signage, patches, print) in one ink. Retired, then
revived precisely because it works everywhere. **Mechanism:** a mark engineered for
reproduction across touchpoints and one-color output ages and scales better than a
detailed emblem. **Borrow:** design for the worst reproduction case first
(one ink, tiny size); if it survives there it survives everywhere.

## 5. Dropbox 2017 rebrand (Collins) — flexible system over fixed logo
Dropbox kept a simplified box glyph but built a deliberately *expandable* system:
a large custom type (Sharp Grotesk) with many widths, a loud variable color palette,
and collage-style flexibility — so the brand looks alive across countless surfaces.
**Mechanism:** define rules that permit variation (a kit of parts) rather than one
frozen lockup, giving range without losing recognition. **Borrow:** ship a
"kit of parts" + usage rules so the brand flexes per channel yet stays coherent.

## 6. Liquid Death — verbal identity as the moat
A water brand whose differentiation is almost entirely *voice* (heavy-metal "Murder
Your Thirst" irreverence) plus an ownable tallboy can and skull device — in a
category of interchangeable bottles. **Mechanism:** when the product is a commodity,
a distinctive verbal identity + one ownable physical asset create the entire
distinction. **Borrow:** in undifferentiated categories, lead with voice and a
single ownable asset, not a prettier logo.
