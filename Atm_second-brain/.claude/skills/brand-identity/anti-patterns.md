# Brand Identity Anti-Patterns (detectable failure modes + fix)

## 1. Visuals before strategy
**Tell:** the work opens with logo sketches, fonts, or a Pinterest board; no one
can state the positioning sentence or the audience in plain words.
**Fix:** stop designing. Write positioning, audience, personality (with rejected
opposites), and differentiation first. Every pixel must trace to one of them.

## 2. Logo that dies at small size or in mono
**Tell:** the mark is illegible at favicon scale, or turns to mud in solid black /
knockout white; it depends on a gradient or fine detail to read.
**Fix:** design the worst case first (one ink, ~16px). Provide a monogram/icon and
favicon-scale variant. If it needs color to be recognizable, it isn't a logo yet.

## 3. Trend-chasing (the "blanding" / generic-tech look)
**Tell:** geometric sans wordmark + abstract gradient blob + the season's palette;
indistinguishable from 50 other brands; the choice is "this looks current."
**Fix:** anchor decisions to the brand's own attributes and differentiation, not the
trend. Ask "what here could ONLY be us?" If the answer is nothing, restart.

## 4. Inconsistent application across touchpoints
**Tell:** the website, deck, app, and social all use slightly different blues, type
sizes, or logo versions; each surface was designed in isolation.
**Fix:** encode color/type/space as named tokens (one source of truth) and ship
guidelines with do/don't + ready assets. Audit live touchpoints against the tokens.

## 5. No distinctive brand asset
**Tell:** remove the logo and nothing identifies the brand; no ownable color, shape,
character, sound, or device.
**Fix:** create at least one distinctive asset and apply it relentlessly. Test it on
fame (do people link it to you) and uniqueness (does it belong to anyone else).

## 6. Copying a competitor's equity
**Tell:** the mark, palette, or voice "reminds you of" a category leader; it was
benchmarked into a look-alike.
**Fix:** map what the whole category shares, then diverge on purpose. Borrowing a
rival's equity confuses customers, builds their brand, and risks legal exposure.

## 7. Voice as one flat setting (or none)
**Tell:** guidelines say "friendly and professional" and stop; error messages,
launches, and apologies all sound identical; or there's no verbal identity at all.
**Fix:** define a constant voice plus a tone *range* mapped to emotional contexts,
and a lexicon of words to use and ban. Treat words as ownable as the logo.

## 8. Style guide as a logo-placement PDF (no system, no why)
**Tell:** the "brand book" is clearspace + a color swatch and nothing else; extenders
copy surface and the brand drifts the moment someone new touches it.
**Fix:** document the strategy, the *because* behind decisions, do/don't examples,
tokens, and application rules — guidelines that teach the system, not just police it.
