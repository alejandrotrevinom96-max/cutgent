# Sources

These are reputable *general-education* references for legal literacy. They are
starting points for understanding, not substitutes for a licensed attorney advising
on your facts and jurisdiction.

## General legal education
- **Nolo** (nolo.com) — plain-language explainers on contracts, IP, business
  entities, and small-business law (US-oriented).
- **Cornell Legal Information Institute (LII)** — free statutes, regulations, and
  the Wex legal dictionary (US law).
- **Your jurisdiction's small-business / government portal** — e.g. US SBA
  (sba.gov), UK gov.uk, etc. for entity-formation and compliance basics.

## Copyright, trademark, patent
- **U.S. Copyright Office** (copyright.gov) — what copyright covers, registration,
  the (narrow, fact-specific) fair-use index.
- **U.S. Patent and Trademark Office** (uspto.gov) — trademark and patent basics.
- **WIPO** (wipo.int) — international IP basics; reminder that copyright/trademark
  rules and terms differ by country.

## Licensing
- **Creative Commons** (creativecommons.org) — how CC licenses work; license
  chooser and the meaning of BY / NC / ND / SA terms.
- The actual license text of any asset you use (stock, music, footage, font) — read
  the specific terms; libraries differ widely.

## Privacy / terms
- Official text of applicable regimes (e.g. GDPR via the EU, CCPA/CPRA via the
  California AG) and reputable summaries — but confirm what applies to *you*.

## Freshness and jurisdiction caveat
Law changes and is jurisdiction-specific; statutes, case law, and license terms can
be amended at any time, and most US-centric sources do not state other countries'
rules. Treat everything here as point-in-time and re-verify against the current law
of the **relevant jurisdiction** before acting. The `personal/` layer (the user's
own jurisdiction, entity, and actual signed agreements) supersedes everything here
when present. For any consequential decision, consult a licensed attorney.
