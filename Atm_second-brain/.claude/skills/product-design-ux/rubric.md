# Product & UX Rubric (binary — every box must hold before a design ships)

- [ ] The user's Job To Be Done is stated as situation + motivation + outcome, independent of any specific solution.
- [ ] The target user and their context (situation, device, constraints, prior knowledge) are named — not "everyone."
- [ ] One primary behavioral success metric is defined, plus at least one guardrail metric, before designing.
- [ ] The research method fits the question (interviews/contextual inquiry for problems, usability tests for designs, analytics/A-B for magnitude).
- [ ] Findings are grounded in observed behavior, not only stated opinion or hypothetical "would you" answers.
- [ ] The end-to-end flow is mapped from trigger to outcome, and at least one step was removed or justified.
- [ ] Every screen has empty, loading, error, success, and relevant edge states designed (zero/one/many, offline, denied).
- [ ] Each action gives immediate, legible feedback; nothing leaves the user guessing whether it worked.
- [ ] The design favors recognition over recall and uses sensible defaults / progressive disclosure to limit cognitive load.
- [ ] Destructive or costly actions are prevented or confirmed and reversible (undo/exit available).
- [ ] The flow was evaluated against all 10 Nielsen heuristics in a walkthrough.
- [ ] At least one prototype was tested with real users on real tasks, top issues fixed, and the fix retested.
- [ ] Build candidates are prioritized by impact × reach ÷ effort tied to the success metric, and the smallest valuable slice is chosen.
- [ ] No dark patterns: no hidden costs, forced continuity, confirmshaming, fake scarcity, pre-checked opt-ins, or trap cancellation.
- [ ] `personal/` recall has been consulted; any conflict with this generic advice is flagged and personal/ wins.
