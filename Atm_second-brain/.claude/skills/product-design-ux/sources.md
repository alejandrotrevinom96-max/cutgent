# Sources

## Canonical (core method)
- Don Norman — *The Design of Everyday Things*. Affordances, signifiers, mapping,
  feedback, forgiving design, error prevention; the foundation of interaction design.
- Steve Krug — *Don't Make Me Think*. Usability as self-evidence; cheap, frequent testing.
- Jakob Nielsen — *Usability Engineering* and the **10 Usability Heuristics**; the
  "5 users find ~85% of problems" discount-usability finding.
- Clayton Christensen, Bob Moesta, Karen Dillon — *Competing Against Luck* / Jobs To
  Be Done. Framing demand around the progress the user seeks in a situation.
- Marty Cagan — *Inspired* (and *Empowered*). Product discovery vs delivery,
  outcome over output, empowered product teams.
- Teresa Torres — *Continuous Discovery Habits*. Opportunity-solution trees,
  weekly customer touchpoints, assumption testing.
- Erika Hall — *Just Enough Research*. Matching research method to the question;
  behavior over opinion.
- Alan Cooper — *About Face* / *The Inmates Are Running the Asylum*. Goal-directed
  design, personas, interaction design fundamentals.

## Laws, heuristics & frameworks
- Fitts's law (target size/distance), Hick's law (choice/decision time), Miller's
  ~7±2 chunking, Tesler's law of conservation of complexity, Jakob's law (users
  expect your site to work like the others they know). See *Laws of UX* (Jon Yablonski).
- RICE / ICE prioritization (Reach × Impact × Confidence ÷ Effort) — Intercom.
- Sean Ellis PMF survey ("how disappointed if you could no longer use this").
- Harry Brignull — *deceptive.design* (dark-patterns taxonomy) for what to avoid.
- WCAG / inclusive-design principles for accessible interaction (visual conformance
  details live in the web-design pack).

## Note on freshness
Platform conventions (mobile/OS interaction patterns, component norms) and analytics
benchmarks drift; treat externally-ingested case studies and metrics as point-in-time
and re-verify before relying on them. The `personal/` layer (the user's own users,
product, research, metrics, and prior decisions) supersedes everything here when present.
