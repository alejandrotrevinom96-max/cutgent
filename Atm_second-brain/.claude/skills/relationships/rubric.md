# Relationships Rubric (binary — each box passes or it doesn't)

- [ ] The reflective-support boundary is surfaced: this is education/reflection, NOT couples or family therapy.
- [ ] A safety/abuse referral path is named whenever there is any sign of abuse, coercive control, or threat to safety (refer to professionals; do not coach managing an abuser).
- [ ] `personal/` recall has been consulted; any conflict with generic advice is flagged and personal/ wins.
- [ ] The frame is "us vs. the problem," not "me vs. you" — the aim is understanding/connection, not winning.
- [ ] The recurring cycle/pattern is named, not just the triggering incident.
- [ ] The softer feeling and the unmet need under each person's surface reaction are identified.
- [ ] The other person's perspective is sought and reflected back before pushing the user's own case.
- [ ] Validation of the other person's experience precedes any problem-solving or rebuttal.
- [ ] At least one need is expressed as "I feel X when Y because I need Z" — observation + feeling + need + request, with no blame or character attack.
- [ ] The startup is soft and specific (no harsh/global opening), and at least one repair attempt is available to make or accept.
- [ ] None of the Four Horsemen are present (no criticism, contempt, defensiveness, or stonewalling); contempt especially is absent.
- [ ] Any boundary is stated as a limit on the user's OWN behavior with a follow-through, not as a demand for the other person to change.
- [ ] Repair after rupture is treated as the goal (name your part, acknowledge impact), rather than avoiding conflict entirely.
- [ ] The user takes appropriate accountability AND keeps their own needs on the table (no self-erasure, fawning, or over-apologizing).
- [ ] Mind-reading is avoided: assumptions about the other person's intent are checked, not asserted as fact.
- [ ] If the situation is entrenched, clinical, or unsafe, routing to a licensed therapist or safety resource is recommended rather than continued coaching.
