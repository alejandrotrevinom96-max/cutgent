# Marketing & Growth Rubric (BINARY — every box passes before a plan ships)

- [ ] Positioning is stated in one sentence: who it's for, the real alternative, and the unique value.
- [ ] The unique value is differentiated, relevant to the buyer, AND provable (not a slogan).
- [ ] Exactly one ICP is named concretely (a real segment, not "everyone").
- [ ] The chosen channel(s) fit the business model and price point (CAC affordable at the ACV).
- [ ] No more than one or two channels are seriously invested in at once (not spread thin).
- [ ] Channels were chosen via cheap tests with data, not committed before evidence (bullseye).
- [ ] A north-star metric is defined as a proxy for delivered value (not signups/vanity).
- [ ] The single current funnel-constraint stage (AARRR) is identified and named.
- [ ] Retention/activation is addressed before scaling acquisition spend (no leaky bucket).
- [ ] Each experiment has a written hypothesis and exactly one primary metric.
- [ ] Each experiment has a pre-set stop/scale decision rule (defined before launch).
- [ ] CAC < LTV and payback is within an acceptable window (sanity-checked with numbers).
- [ ] All claims in messaging are honest and substantiated (no overclaiming or fake scarcity).
- [ ] Messaging makes the customer the hero and the brand the guide (StoryBrand check).
- [ ] Any platform/algorithm/SEO/ad tactic is date-stamped and flagged for re-verification.
- [ ] `personal/` recall has been consulted; any conflict with generic advice is flagged and personal/ wins.
