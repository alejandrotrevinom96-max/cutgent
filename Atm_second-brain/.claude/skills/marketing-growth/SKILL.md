# Marketing & Growth

A binary, sequenced method. **Whenever `personal/` recall returns the user's real
context — product, ICP, current positioning, channels tried, CAC/payback, brand
voice — that OVERRIDES the generic moves below.** Surface the conflict, then follow
`personal/`. Growth without positioning is paid noise; positioning without a chosen
channel reaches no one; channels without retention is a leaky bucket. In order:

## 1. Positioning first
State it in one sentence before anything else: **who it's for** (one concrete
segment), **the real alternative** (a competitor, a spreadsheet, doing nothing),
**the unique value** (the one thing you do better that they care about, with proof —
differentiated, relevant, AND provable). Use Dunford's process (alternatives ->
unique attributes -> value -> who cares most -> market frame). Positioning is a
choice, not a tagline.

## 2. One ICP
Pick one ideal customer profile and over-index on them. Breadth dilutes the message
and the channel math. Expand later.

## 3. Channels that fit the model
Don't spread thin — win with one or two at a time. **Channel/model fit (Balfour):**
a channel must fit your price point and business model. **Bullseye (Traction):**
brainstorm channels -> pick a few -> cheap tests -> double down on the winner.
**CAC/payback sanity:** viable only if CAC < LTV with acceptable payback (~<12mo for
most SMB/B2B).

## 4. Funnel — AARRR, retention before acquisition
Acquisition -> Activation -> Retention -> Revenue -> Referral. Pick the ONE
constraint stage. **Fix retention before pouring money into acquisition.** Define a
**north-star metric** (proxy for delivered value, not signups) and the activation
"aha" moment, then work the constraint.

## 5. Experiments — one metric each
Each: a written hypothesis, ONE primary metric, a defined audience, and a stop/scale
rule set in advance. Kill losers fast; double down on winners. Velocity of validated
learning beats any single big bet.

## 6. Content / SEO basics
Target real search intent (not just volume); earn topical authority (depth on a
cluster); technical hygiene (crawlable, fast, titled, linked). **Date-stamp** any
platform/algorithm-specific tactic — these decay.

## 7. Brand as a compounding asset
Brand lowers CAC over time: consistent message, voice, promise, kept honest. **No
dishonest claims** — overclaiming wins one sale and loses retention + referral +
trust. StoryBrand: the customer is the hero, you are the guide.
