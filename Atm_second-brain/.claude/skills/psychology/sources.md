# Sources — Psychology

All cited for psychology **education, not clinical psychology, diagnosis, or
therapy.** Prefer well-replicated, large-effect findings; read even the canon with
the replication note in mind.

## Cognition & judgment
- **Daniel Kahneman — *Thinking, Fast and Slow*.** Two-system thinking, heuristics
  and biases. *Caveat: some chapters (esp. social priming) rest on findings that
  didn't hold up — Kahneman acknowledged this. Read critically.*

## Social influence
- **Robert Cialdini — *Influence*.** Six principles; use ethically, never to manipulate.

## Motivation
- **Deci & Ryan — Self-Determination Theory.** Autonomy/competence/relatedness;
  intrinsic vs extrinsic; overjustification. Among the better-replicated frameworks.

## Habits & behavior change
- **Charles Duhigg — *The Power of Habit*.** Cue-routine-reward. *Popular synthesis.*
- **James Clear — *Atomic Habits*.** Environment/identity-based design. *Self-help,
  not primary research — use the mechanisms.*
- **BJ Fogg — *Tiny Habits* / B=MAP.** Behavior = Motivation x Ability x Prompt.

## Mindset
- **Carol Dweck — *Mindset*.** Influential. *Replication caveat: meta-analyses find
  growth-mindset interventions real but small on average, concentrated in specific
  groups — not the universal transformation pop versions claim.*

## Replication crisis (read this)
- **Open Science Collaboration (2015), *Science*.** ~a third to a half of studies
  replicated — why this pack weights replication and effect size.
- **Failed/weak claims to flag, not repeat as fact:** ego depletion; power posing;
  many social-priming effects; strong "learning styles"; facial-feedback (mixed).
  **MBTI is not validated.**

## Scope note
For anything clinical — a possible disorder, persistent distress, trauma, self-harm,
medication, interpreting symptoms — defer to a licensed professional. Crisis: US 988
Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (call/text 988); your local emergency number.
