# Rubric — Psychology (BINARY)

Psychology education, not clinical psychology, diagnosis, or therapy.

## Framing the question
- [ ] The question is classified as cognitive, behavioral, social, or emotional.
- [ ] At least one specific, well-supported model is named (not a vague "studies show").
- [ ] The personal/ layer was consulted and given precedence over generic models.

## Evidence quality (the point)
- [ ] Replication status of the cited finding is considered/stated (robust vs contested).
- [ ] Effect size / uncertainty is weighted, not just the headline name of the effect.
- [ ] The claim used is NOT a known failed/weak one presented as fact (ego depletion, power posing, social priming, strong learning styles, facial-feedback-as-strong).
- [ ] No MBTI/enneagram-style instrument is treated as scientifically valid.
- [ ] No single study is overreached into a universal law.

## Applying the right model
- [ ] For habits: environment/behavior design (cue-routine-reward or B=MAP / tiny habits), not willpower-only advice.
- [ ] For motivation: intrinsic motivation / SDT needs (autonomy, competence, relatedness) are considered, not just rewards/pressure.
- [ ] For social reads: attribution is checked (situation vs disposition; FAE guarded against).
- [ ] Influence principles, if used, are used to understand/act ethically — not to manipulate.
- [ ] For emotion: a supported regulation strategy (e.g., reappraisal) is offered as education, not therapy.

## Boundaries
- [ ] The clinical disclaimer is present (education, not diagnosis/therapy; route mental-health concerns to professionals).
- [ ] Crisis signals would trigger stop-and-route to professional/crisis resources.
- [ ] Scope is kept distinct from counsel (support/reflection) and decision-making (choosing under uncertainty).
- [ ] No diagnosis, pathologizing, or armchair labeling of the user or others.
