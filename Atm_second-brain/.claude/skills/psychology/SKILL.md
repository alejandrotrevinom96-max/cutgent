# Psychology

A method for applying psychology to real questions — your own behavior, other
people, habits, motivation, social dynamics — judged against the binary rubric,
NOT a "you are an expert psychologist" persona. The whole point is honesty about
what the evidence actually supports.

## DISCLAIMER (state to the user)
This is **psychology EDUCATION, NOT clinical psychology, diagnosis, treatment, or
therapy.** It does not diagnose, interpret symptoms, or replace a licensed
psychologist, psychiatrist, or therapist. Do not armchair-diagnose yourself or
anyone. **Route mental-health concerns to a professional.** On any sign of crisis —
self-harm, suicidal thoughts, harm to others, abuse, inability to stay safe — stop
and surface professional/crisis resources (US: 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) and
the local emergency number.

## Boundary with neighboring packs
- **counsel** = supportive conversation, listening, reflection. Use when someone
  wants to be *heard*.
- **psychology** (here) = the *model* of how minds/behavior operate, applied to
  understand and act. Use when the question is "why does this happen / how do I
  change it."
- **decision-making** owns *choosing under uncertainty* (base rates, EV, biases as
  decision errors). Cross-reference; don't re-derive it here.

## PRECEDENCE: personal layer wins
The user's `personal/` layer (their own patterns, history, what has and hasn't
worked, goals), via recall, **OVERRIDES the generic models here.** A textbook
effect is a default prior, not the authority on this person; n=1 lived evidence
about *them* can outweigh a group average.

## Method (in order)
1. **Classify the question.** Cognitive, behavioral, social, or emotional? This
   picks the model.
2. **Reach for a well-supported model** for that class (below). Name it.
3. **Check evidence quality before applying.** Well-replicated? Effect size large
   and reliable, or small/noisy? Is it a famous claim that FAILED to replicate?
   Prefer robust findings; flag weak ones.
4. **Apply it to the concrete situation**, then consult the personal layer.
5. **Respect the clinical boundary.** If it shades into a condition, stop and route
   to a professional.

## Models by question type
**Behavior / habits**
- Cue -> routine -> reward loop. Make the cue obvious, the routine easy, the reward
  immediate.
- Fogg: **Behavior = Motivation x Ability x Prompt (B=MAP).** If a behavior isn't
  happening, one is missing — usually make it *easier*. Tiny Habits: shrink it,
  anchor to an existing routine, celebrate immediately.
- **Design the environment.** Behavior follows least friction far more reliably
  than willpower.

**Motivation**
- **Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan):** durable motivation needs **autonomy,
  competence, relatedness.** Intrinsic beats extrinsic; heavy rewards can undermine
  intrinsic interest (overjustification).

**Cognitive (briefly)**
- Two-system thinking; common biases (confirmation, availability, anchoring). Keep
  short — **decision-making owns this.** Correct with the outside view / base rates.

**Social**
- **Cialdini's principles** (reciprocity, commitment/consistency, social proof,
  authority, liking, scarcity) — to *understand* and act ethically, never manipulate.
- **Fundamental attribution error:** check situation before judging disposition.
- **Conformity** (Asch-style): groups bend individual judgment.

**Emotion**
- **Reappraisal** (reframing the meaning) is well-supported and generally healthier
  than suppression. Name the emotion; re-evaluate the appraisal. (Education, not therapy.)

## Honesty rules (the replication crisis)
- Psychology has a serious **replication crisis** (Open Science Collaboration 2015:
  ~a third to a half replicated). Default to well-replicated, large-effect findings;
  treat single splashy studies as weak.
- **Flag claims that failed/weak, don't repeat as fact:** ego depletion; **power
  posing**; many **social-priming** effects; strong **"learning styles"**;
  **facial-feedback** (mixed). **MBTI is not validated.**
- Report effect size and uncertainty over headlines. No pathologizing or diagnosis.
