# Decision-Making Anti-Patterns

- **Resulting.** "It worked, so it was right" over one sample. *Fix:* evaluate the
  process and information at the time; separate luck from judgment.
- **Whether-or-not framing.** "Should I do X, yes/no?" *Fix:* force >=3 different
  alternatives, incl. do-nothing and a reversible test.
- **Two-way door treated as one-way.** Weeks of analysis on a cheaply reversible call.
  *Fix:* classify the door; decide fast and test.
- **Inside view only (base-rate neglect).** Confident estimate, no reference class.
  *Fix:* anchor on the base rate, then adjust.
- **Confirmation bias.** Only gathering supporting evidence. *Fix:* state what would
  change your mind first; argue the opposing case.
- **Sunk-cost reasoning.** "We've put in so much to quit now." *Fix:* decide only on
  future costs/benefits; would I start this today?
- **Anchoring.** One early number frames everything. *Fix:* estimate independently
  first, or re-anchor on base rates.
- **Ignoring the survivable-downside test.** Chasing positive EV without checking the
  tail. *Fix:* name the downside; a bet you can't survive is a no.
- **Undocumented decision.** Made in your head; no learning. *Fix:* write the
  decision, assumptions, expectation; review at the outcome.
