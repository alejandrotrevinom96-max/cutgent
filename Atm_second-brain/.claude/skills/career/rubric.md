# Career Readiness Rubric (binary — every box must hold before a move)

- [ ] Direction is stated as the concrete overlap of strengths × what's valued × what energizes — not a job title or a vibe.
- [ ] Positioning is one sentence naming a specific audience, a specific outcome, and your edge — and could NOT describe a thousand people.
- [ ] A coherent narrative through-line connects past moves to where you're going and why now (pivots framed as accumulation, not wandering).
- [ ] Every resume bullet leads with a quantified result and outcome (action + metric + so-what), not a duty or responsibility.
- [ ] The resume / application is tailored to the specific role: it mirrors the role's language and leads with what that role values.
- [ ] Claims are backed by pointable proof (portfolio, shipped work, writing, references, numbers) — not adjectives alone.
- [ ] 6–8 STAR stories with numbers are pre-banked and mapped to likely interview questions (conflict, failure, leadership, ambiguity, impact).
- [ ] Crisp, true answers exist for "why you" and "why them," plus researched, judgment-revealing questions to ask the interviewer.
- [ ] Leverage is real and built in advance: a credible alternative (option / standing offer / runway) plus reputation plus a scarce skill — not summoned in a crisis.
- [ ] The network is being maintained give-first and continuously (weak ties cultivated), not activated only when job-hunting.
- [ ] Any offer or transition is evaluated on total comp + growth + people + optionality against the stated direction and BATNA — never on salary alone.
- [ ] Comp number/mechanics are handed to the negotiation pack; no compensation tactics are duplicated here.
- [ ] No fabricated credentials, titles, experience, or inflated metrics — every claim is truthful.
- [ ] `personal/` recall AND personal/ wins have been consulted; the user's goals, history, constraints, and past wins override generic advice, and any conflict is flagged with personal/ winning.
