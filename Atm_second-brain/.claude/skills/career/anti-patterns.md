# Career Anti-Patterns (detectable failure modes + fix)

## 1. Duties, not impact, on the resume
**Tell:** bullets start with "Responsible for…", "Worked on…", "Helped with…"; no
numbers anywhere.
**Fix:** rewrite each as action verb + metric + outcome ("Cut X 40%, unblocking Y").
The reader buys results and scope, not your job description. If no metric exists, use a
before/after, a scale, or a concrete artifact.

## 2. Generic positioning
**Tell:** you describe yourself by title ("software engineer," "marketer," "consultant")
or in words that fit a thousand people.
**Fix:** write the one-sentence position — specific audience + specific outcome + your
edge — and narrow until it's no longer generic. Be *the* person for a problem.

## 3. Networking only when job-hunting
**Tell:** your network goes silent for years, then you message everyone the week you
need a referral.
**Fix:** tend relationships continuously and give first (intros, help, useful signal)
with no scoreboard. Cultivate weak ties before you need them; warm beats cold.

## 4. No leverage / no options
**Tell:** you "can't" leave or ask because you have no alternative; one job is your
whole safety net.
**Fix:** build leverage in advance — runway, a rare valued skill, reputation, and a
credible alternative. You only negotiate and walk from strength you built earlier.

## 5. Untailored applications
**Tell:** the same resume and cover letter go to every posting; the role's keywords and
priorities don't appear.
**Fix:** tailor per role — mirror its language, lead with what *that* role values, and
cut what's irrelevant. An untailored application signals low interest and low effort.

## 6. Evaluating offers on salary alone
**Tell:** you compare two roles purely by base salary; growth, manager, equity, and
optionality aren't on the table.
**Fix:** evaluate total comp + growth + people + optionality against your direction and
BATNA. A lower-salary role that builds rare career capital often wins long-term.

## 7. Fabricating or inflating experience
**Tell:** a title, credential, dates, or metric on the resume isn't literally true; a
STAR story describes work you didn't do.
**Fix:** never fabricate. Frame *real* achievement in its strongest truthful light.
Lies collapse on a reference check or on the job, and torch reputation — your scarcest
career asset.

## 8. Drifting with no direction
**Tell:** moves are reactive (whoever calls, whatever pays now), with no through-line
and no overlap of strengths × value × energy.
**Fix:** define direction as the concrete overlap, then make each move build career
capital or set up the next. Prototype with small reversible experiments instead of
agonizing or drifting.
