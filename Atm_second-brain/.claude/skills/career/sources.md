# Sources

## Canonical (core method)
- Cal Newport — *So Good They Can't Ignore You*. Career capital over "follow your
  passion"; rare and valuable skills buy autonomy, meaning, and leverage.
- Bill Burnett & Dave Evans — *Designing Your Life*. Prototyping a career through small
  reversible experiments; reframing "dysfunctional beliefs"; multiple Odyssey plans.
- Richard N. Bolles — *What Color Is Your Parachute?*. Self-inventory of skills and
  interests, targeted (non-spray-and-pray) job search, informational interviewing.
- Reid Hoffman & Ben Casnocha — *The Start-up of You*. Permanent beta, building a
  network and a portfolio of competitive assets, intelligent risk and ABZ planning.
- Mark Granovetter — "The Strength of Weak Ties" (1973). Why acquaintances, not close
  friends, surface most opportunities.
- Keith Ferrazzi — *Never Eat Alone*. Give-first, relationship-driven networking.

## Positioning & narrative
- Al Ries & Jack Trout — *Positioning*. Owning a specific position in the audience's
  mind; differentiation over being "better."
- Resume/portfolio craft: result-first bullets (action verb + metric + outcome) and
  per-role tailoring (industry-standard practice; cf. Google/McKinsey resume guidance).

## Interview
- Gayle Laakmann McDowell — *Cracking the Coding Interview* (software roles) and
  *Cracking the PM Interview* (product roles). Domain-specific interview preparation.
- STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) — standard structure for behavioral
  interview answers.

## Compensation
- Mechanics (anchoring, BATNA, concessions, who opens) live in the **negotiation**
  pack — not duplicated here. This pack frames *what* to ask for and *whether* to take
  a role; negotiation runs the exchange.

## Note on freshness
Market specifics — salary bands, in-demand skills, hiring climate, role norms — are
point-in-time and change fast; they are externally ingested via recall and must be
re-verified before acting (treat as date-stamped). The `personal/` layer (the user's
goals, history, values, constraints, prior offers, and wins) supersedes everything
here when present.
