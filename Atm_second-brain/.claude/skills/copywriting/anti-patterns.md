# Anti-patterns — copywriting

- **Feature dump.** Symptom: a spec list with no outcome. *Fix:* translate each
  feature into the reader's result; lead with the result.
- **We-we copy.** Symptom: every sentence starts with the company. *Fix:* recenter
  on "you" and the reader's desire.
- **Adjective inflation.** Symptom: "revolutionary, seamless, world-class". *Fix:*
  replace with a number or a concrete proof.
- **Two (or five) CTAs.** Symptom: competing asks. *Fix:* one primary action;
  remove or demote the rest.
- **Buried lede.** Symptom: the offer appears in paragraph four. *Fix:* promise in
  the headline/first line.
- **Hedging.** Symptom: "we think this might help you possibly…". *Fix:* commit;
  cut qualifiers.
- **Fake urgency / invented stats.** Symptom: countdown timers that reset,
  made-up percentages. *Fix:* only real scarcity and verifiable numbers.
- **Clever over clear.** Symptom: a pun the reader has to decode. *Fix:* clarity
  first; wit only if it doesn't cost comprehension.
