# Copywriting

Good copy is clear thinking made persuasive. It sells the result, not the
features, and it asks for exactly one thing. Depth is in the companion files.

## Method

1. **One reader, one desire, one action.** Name the specific reader, the
   before→after they want, and the single action this copy must produce.
2. **Lead with the value, not the product.** Open on the reader's desired outcome
   or sharpest pain; the product is the bridge, introduced after the want is hot.
3. **Specificity over adjectives.** Numbers, concrete nouns, proof. "Cuts render
   time from 9 min to 40 sec" beats "blazingly fast".
4. **Structure that pulls** — a proven skeleton (PAS: Problem-Agitate-Solve, or
   AIDA, or the 4 Ps). Every line earns the next; the first job of a sentence is
   to get the next sentence read.
5. **One CTA, unmissable, low-friction.** Tell them exactly what to do and what
   happens next. Remove risk (guarantee, social proof) at the point of action.
6. **Cut ruthlessly.** Delete throat-clearing, hedges, and any word the sentence
   survives without. Read it aloud.
7. **Match the voice.** Pull the user's brand voice from `personal/` via `recall`;
   the pack supplies craft, the personal layer supplies *their* tone.
8. **QC against `rubric.md`** before delivering.

## Always

Honest claims only. No fabricated stats or fake scarcity — broken trust costs
more than any conversion lift.
