# Exemplars — copywriting

- **Ogilvy, "At 60 miles an hour the loudest noise… comes from the electric
  clock."** *Lesson:* one specific, sensory proof point out-sells a page of
  adjectives.
- **Apple, "1,000 songs in your pocket."** *Lesson:* translate a spec
  (5GB) into the reader's outcome.
- **Basecamp/37signals landing pages.** *Lesson:* plain-spoken benefit + a single
  CTA + honest tone converts without hype.
- **Sugarman's "slippery slide."** *Lesson:* the sole purpose of the first
  sentence is to get the second read — engineer momentum.
- **Wait But Why / Stripe docs intros.** *Lesson:* clarity is persuasion; make
  the reader feel smart, not sold-to.

Use: pick the closest archetype and borrow its *mechanism* (specific proof,
spec→outcome translation, momentum), not its words.
