# Anti-patterns — productivity

## Vague tasks ("wishes")
"Project X," "taxes," "fix the thing" — no next action → avoidance. **Fix:** rewrite
as the next concrete action ("download tax forms from portal").

## Many inboxes / capture leaks
Tasks scattered across flags, notes, stars, head → things fall through; you trust
none of it. **Fix:** one trusted inbox; process to zero.

## Urgency theater / firefighting
Reacting all day; the inbox sets the agenda. Urgent ≠ important. **Fix:**
Eisenhower-sort; schedule important-not-urgent before reactive work.

## To-do list with no calendar
A 30-item list, no time assigned; you overcommit and slide. **Fix:** timeblock —
assign tasks to real hours.

## Multitasking / unlimited WIP
Ten things "in progress," nothing finished; switching costs are real. **Fix:** cap
WIP; single-task; finish before starting.

## No deep-work block / always reachable
Day fragmented by meetings and pings; never 90 uninterrupted minutes. **Fix:**
schedule and defend a deep block; notifications off.

## Saying yes to everything
Over-commitment guarantees dropped balls and shallow output. **Fix:** default to no
for low-leverage asks; do fewer things well.

## Skipping the weekly review
The system works two weeks then rots; you revert to head + firefighting. **Fix:** a
fixed weekly review slot is non-negotiable — the keystone habit.

## Productivity-tool tinkering
App-switching, elaborate templates, setup videos substitute for the work. **Fix:**
one boring tool; cap setup; let the rubric judge it.

## Hustle / no recovery
Maximizing hours, treating rest as waste; output is energy-bound, not hour-bound.
**Fix:** protect sleep, breaks, downtime as part of the system.

## Collecting into the vault without doing
Endless clipping; the vault becomes a graveyard. **Fix:** process captures into
next-actions/PARA — storage isn't doing.
