# Productivity

A focus and execution method for doing the few high-leverage things that matter,
calmly and reliably — not for doing more things faster. The aim is a trusted system
that holds your commitments so your head is free to think.

## Personal layer overrides this

Before applying anything below, recall the user's `personal/` context: goals and
projects, hard commitments and deadlines, energy/chronotype, and constraints.
**Personal context wins.** If they think best at night, don't prescribe 5am deep
work. If their real bottleneck is over-committing, prioritize "say no." Say so when
you override.

## The method

1. **Capture everything, one inbox.** Every task/idea/commitment into a single
   trusted place the moment it appears. Open loops in your head create anxiety and
   get dropped. One inbox is ideal.
2. **Clarify into concrete next-actions.** Process the inbox to zero regularly. For
   each item: actionable? If yes, the *very next physical action* ("email Sam the
   draft"); if <2 min, do it now; else defer (with a date) or delegate. If not:
   trash, someday/maybe, or reference.
3. **Organize by PARA.** Projects (active, with an end), Areas (ongoing), Resources
   (interests), Archive (inactive). Use the vault's PARA structure — don't invent a
   parallel one or duplicate vault-conventions.
4. **Prioritize by leverage, not urgency.** Each day pick the 1–3 tasks that move a
   goal. Eisenhower-sort; kill/downgrade urgent-but-unimportant noise. Importance
   comes from your goals, not the inbox's loudest item.
5. **Timeblock and protect deep work.** Schedule concrete blocks (at least one
   distraction-free deep-work block for the hardest task) and work the calendar.
   Defend the block: no meetings, no notifications.
6. **Single-task with WIP limits.** One thing at a time; cap work-in-progress.
   "Started" is not "done." Finish before you start more.
7. **Manage energy, not just time.** Hardest work in peak hours; batch shallow work
   into low-energy windows; build in real recovery.
8. **Say no / limit scope.** Every yes is a no to something else. Do fewer things,
   at a humane pace, done well (slow productivity).
9. **Weekly review.** Once a week: clear inboxes, review projects and calendar,
   update next-actions, check progress against goals. The keystone habit — skip it
   and the system rots.

## Defaults

- When overwhelmed: stop, do a full brain-dump capture, then clarify next-actions —
  the overwhelm is usually un-captured open loops, not too little time.
- Default to fewer commitments and one daily deep-work block.
- A task without a concrete next-action is a wish; rewrite it.
- If you skip exactly one practice, don't let it be the weekly review.
