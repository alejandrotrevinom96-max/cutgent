# Health & Fitness

A method for building training, nutrition, and recovery plans that move the needle,
judged against the binary rubric — not a personal-trainer persona.

## DISCLAIMER (state to the user)
This is **general fitness and nutrition education, NOT medical, clinical,
diagnostic, or treatment advice.** It does not diagnose, prescribe, or replace a
physician, registered dietitian, or physical therapist. **Consult a doctor before
starting a program**, especially with any health condition, if pregnant, on
medication, or over ~40/sedentary. **Stop and see a professional for pain beyond
normal soreness, injury, dizziness, chest pain, or shortness of breath.**

## PRECEDENCE: personal layer wins
The user's `personal/` layer (goals, deadline, schedule, equipment, training age and
history, injuries/limitations, food preferences/allergies, bodyweight, prior
programs), via recall, **OVERRIDES the generic defaults here.**

## Method (in order)
1. **Define the goal + commit to adherence.** One primary goal (strength /
   hypertrophy / endurance / general health) with a timeline; design for what the
   person will actually do consistently. Adherence beats the "optimal" plan they quit.
2. **Progressive overload.** Do more over time (load, reps, sets, quality) — not
   random "muscle confusion." Track it.
3. **Prioritize compound movements** (squat, hinge, horizontal/vertical push & pull,
   carries); add isolation as accessories.
4. **Train each muscle ~2x/week** (full-body or upper/lower) for most non-advanced
   trainees.
5. **Volume in a sensible range** — roughly ~10-20 hard sets/muscle/week for
   hypertrophy (start near MEV, progress toward MAV, deload before MRV). Strength:
   lower reps, higher intensity, more rest.
6. **Nutrition for the goal.** Protein ~1.6-2.2 g/kg/day (general guidance). Total
   calories drive direction (surplus ~+10% to gain, deficit ~-10-20% to lose,
   maintenance to maintain/recomp). Order: calories -> protein -> fats/carbs ->
   timing/supplements.
7. **Sleep + recovery ARE training.** ~7-9 h sleep is foundational — adaptation
   happens during recovery, not in the gym.
8. **Cardio by zones.** Easy Zone 2 for aerobic base/health plus some higher
   intensity; ACSM-style baseline ~150+ min/week moderate (or 75+ vigorous) + 2
   strength sessions for general health.
9. **Deload + injury caution.** Plan lighter weeks; rising joint pain, stalled
   lifts, and poor sleep mean back off, not push through. Form before load.
10. **Reassess** every few weeks based on progress.

## Plateaus (quick procedure)
Check in order: adherence -> sleep -> calories+protein -> is overload actually
applied? -> volume too low/high -> need a deload -> exercise selection/technique.
Change ONE variable at a time.

## Honesty rules
- Separate well-established principles (energy balance, protein, progressive
  overload, sleep) from debated specifics (exact set counts, meal timing,
  supplements). Most supplements do little; flag the few with evidence (creatine,
  caffeine) as general info, not a recommendation.
- Never diagnose or treat; route pain/conditions to professionals.
- Date-stamp public-guideline specifics (they get revised).
