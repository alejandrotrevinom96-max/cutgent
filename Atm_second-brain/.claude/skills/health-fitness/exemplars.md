# Exemplars — Health & Fitness

General education, not prescriptions. Patterns, not gospel; the personal layer overrides.

## Mark Rippetoe — *Starting Strength* (novice linear progression)
A few compound lifts with linear progression (add a little weight each session while
a novice can still recover). **Why:** maximizes the novice's fastest adaptation window
with the highest-stimulus movements and unambiguous progression. Linear gains stall
past novice; technique matters; not for everyone's joints/goals as-is.

## Renaissance Periodization — volume landmarks
Per-muscle weekly volume around MV / MEV / MAV / MRV: start near MEV, add sets toward
MAV, deload before MRV. **Why:** operationalizes "enough but not too much" volume and
bakes in recovery. The numbers are individual estimates, not laws.

## ACSM / activity guidelines (general health)
~150-300 min/week moderate OR 75-150 vigorous aerobic + >=2 days/week resistance for
all major muscle groups (US Physical Activity Guidelines, 2018). **Why:** evidence-
based population floor for health outcomes. *Date-stamped — guidelines get revised.*

## Progressive overload done right
The same muscle is asked to do measurably more over weeks, tracked in a log, one or
two variables at a time. **Why:** adaptation responds to a progressive challenge;
"muscle confusion" can't apply a measurable overload. Tracking separates a program
from a workout.

## Sleep as the foundation
Treating ~7-9 h as a non-negotiable training input. Hard training on chronic 5-h
sleep yields worse recovery, strength, body composition, and injury risk. **Why:**
adaptation and tissue repair happen during sleep. When progress stalls and the plan
looks fine, check sleep first.
