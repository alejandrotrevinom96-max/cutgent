# Sources — learning

## Books
- Ericsson & Pool — *Peak* (2016). Deliberate practice; debunks the naive
  "10,000-hour rule."
- Brown, Roediger & McDaniel — *Make It Stick* (2014). Retrieval practice, spacing,
  interleaving, desirable difficulties.
- Oakley — *A Mind for Numbers* (2014). Focused vs diffuse modes, chunking, recall
  over rereading (companion to "Learning How to Learn").
- Young — *Ultralearning* (2019). Metalearning, directness, drill, retrieval, feedback.

## Spaced-repetition systems
- SuperMemo (Wozniak) — SM-2 and "20 rules of formulating knowledge."
- Anki manual — now defaults to the FSRS scheduler.

## Foundational research
- Ebbinghaus (1885) — forgetting curve and spacing effect.
- Roediger & Karpicke (2006) — the testing effect.
- Dunlosky et al. (2013) — practice testing and distributed practice rated *high*
  utility; highlighting and rereading rated *low*.

## Honesty note
Retrieval practice, spacing, and interleaving are among the best-replicated findings
in learning science. The **"learning styles"** theory is NOT supported — do not build
a plan around it.
