# Exemplars — image & video

Calibrate taste against why these work, not just that they look nice.

## Image

- **Editorial product hero (e.g. Apple/Aesop style).** One subject, vast negative
  space, single directional key light, hyper-clean edges. *Why:* clarity and
  restraint make the product the only thing to look at.
- **Cinematic portrait (Annie Leibovitz / Peter McKinnon thumb).** Strong
  catchlight in the eyes, subject separated from background by depth and rim
  light, warm/cool color contrast. *Why:* light does the storytelling.
- **Brand poster (Pentagram/Alla Kotova).** Bold type as image, 2–3 color
  palette, intentional asymmetry on a grid. *Why:* hierarchy + constraint reads
  instantly at any size.

## Video

- **Retention-first short (MrBeast cold open).** The payoff or stakes appear in
  the first second; no logo intro. *Why:* the hook is the whole game on social.
- **Seamless match-cut sequence.** Motion or shape carries the eye across the
  cut so transitions feel inevitable. *Why:* continuity keeps attention.
- **Motion-controlled re-animation.** A still or clip re-driven with
  `motion_control` to add deliberate, weighted movement instead of generic pan.
  *Why:* purposeful motion beats Ken Burns drift.

## How to use these

Before generating, name which exemplar archetype the task is closest to, then set
prompt + model + aspect to chase that archetype's *reason for working* (light,
hierarchy, hook), not its surface look.
