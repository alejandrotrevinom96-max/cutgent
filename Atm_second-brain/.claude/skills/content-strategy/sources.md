# Sources

## Canonical (core method)
- Creator-economy operators on packaging & retention — MrBeast's title/thumbnail-first
  process and beat-by-beat retention engineering; widely documented in his and his
  team's public talks and breakdowns.
- Alex Hormozi — *$100M Leads* and his content talks: the atomic-content /
  repurposing / "give until they ask" omnipresence model; volume via repurposing.
- Gary Vaynerchuk — *Jab, Jab, Jab, Right Hook* and the "content pyramid" / pillar-
  to-micro-content repurposing model; platform-native distribution.
- Nicolas Cole & Dickie Bush (Ship 30 for 30) — atomic essays, hook/header craft,
  and writing for short-form attention.
- Robert McKee / story-craft and the curiosity-gap tradition (George Loewenstein's
  information-gap theory) — why open loops and stakes hold attention.

## Platform & empirical (point-in-time)
- Instagram: Adam Mosseri's confirmed ranking signals (watch time as #1 for Reels,
  intro retention past 3s) — official creator guidance, 2025.
- YouTube: Creator Insider / official guidance on average view duration, retention
  curves, and click-through as packaging signals.
- TikTok and YouTube Shorts: published creator guidance on completion rate, re-watch,
  and the first-frame hook.
- Industry retention benchmarks (intro hold, watch-through) from creator-analytics
  publishers — treat specific numbers as approximate and date-stamped.

## Note on freshness
Platform algorithms, ranking signals, format specs, and benchmark numbers are
point-in-time and change frequently — re-verify any platform-specific claim before
acting on it, and prefer the platform's own current creator docs. The `personal/`
layer (the user's niche, voice, audience analytics, and which of *their* posts
actually performed) supersedes everything here when present; their own retention
data is the highest authority.
