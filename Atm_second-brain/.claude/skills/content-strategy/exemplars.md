# Content Strategy Exemplars (borrow the mechanism, not the surface)

## 1. MrBeast — title/thumbnail as the product, then pay it off
Videos are conceived title-and-thumbnail first; the concept exists to make a
clickable, true promise ("I Survived 50 Hours in X"), and the edit front-loads
spectacle and re-hooks at every drop-off point to defend retention.
**Mechanism:** the promise (packaging) is designed before production and the content
is built to deliver it, with retention engineered beat-by-beat. **Borrow:** write
the title/thumbnail/hook first as a true promise; cut anything that doesn't serve
keeping the viewer; plant re-hooks where viewers leave.

## 2. Alex Hormozi — atomic content / "give until they ask"
One long pillar piece (a talk, a stream) is sliced into dozens of platform-native
atoms posted everywhere, each re-cut to its surface, all reinforcing one positioning.
Volume comes from repurposing, not from inventing more ideas.
**Mechanism:** one deep idea → many native surfaces (omnipresence) with a consistent
promise; distribution is a pipeline, not per-post effort. **Borrow:** produce one
thing deeply, then systematically atomize it; keep the angle constant across cuts.

## 3. The "open loop" cold open (used across short-form)
Top short-form opens mid-action or states the payoff/curiosity gap in frame one
("here's why your hook keeps failing") so the viewer must stay to close the loop —
intro retention stays above ~60%, which 5–10×'s reach versus weak holds.
**Mechanism:** a curiosity gap or stakes stated immediately converts the costly
first-3s window. **Borrow:** lead with the unresolved question or the result, not
the setup; delete the intro.

## 4. Recurring named series (e.g. signature segments / "X but Y" formats)
Channels that run a named, repeatable segment train the audience to return *for the
container* and de-risk every new upload — the format is proven, only the topic
changes. **Mechanism:** a recognizable repeatable container builds anticipation and
removes format risk. **Borrow:** turn your best-performing structure into a named
series and run it on a cadence; vary topic, hold the format.

## 5. Content pillars tied to one promise (creator-brand model)
Durable creators run 3–5 pillars (e.g. teach / behind-the-scenes / opinion) that
all ladder to one positioning, so ideation never runs dry and viewers know what
they're subscribing to. **Mechanism:** a bounded pillar set makes content
infinite-yet-coherent. **Borrow:** define your pillars explicitly; reject ideas
that fit none, because off-pillar content dilutes the promise.

## 6. Platform-native re-cut over raw cross-post
The same idea performs when re-grammared per platform (vertical + burned captions +
fast cuts for Reels/Shorts/TikTok; thumbnail + chaptered title for long YouTube;
hook line + scannable body for X/LinkedIn) and dies when one export is dumped
everywhere. **Mechanism:** each platform rewards its own native form factor.
**Borrow:** repurpose by re-editing to the destination's grammar and CTA, never by
uploading the identical file across surfaces.
