# Content Strategy

A binary, ship-and-learn procedure for earning attention you can keep. Generic
best practice is the floor. **Whenever `personal/` recall returns the user's real
context — their niche, voice, audience, which past posts hit or flopped, their
platform analytics — that OVERRIDES the generic moves below.** Surface the conflict,
then follow personal/. A creator's own retention data beats any rule of thumb.

## 1. Audience + positioning (before any content)

- **One person, one promise.** Name the specific viewer (not "everyone") and the
  recurring promise every piece keeps: what they get, why from you. Vague audience
  = vague content = no compounding.
- **Differentiated angle.** What can you say that the niche's incumbents can't or
  won't? Positioning is a wedge, not a topic list.

## 2. Promise / format fit

- Match the idea to the format that delivers it best: a transformation wants
  before/after; a how-to wants a numbered build; a take wants a talking-head or
  text post. Forcing a format on the wrong idea kills it.
- **Platform-native, not cross-posted raw.** Each platform has a native grammar
  (vertical + captions + fast cuts for Shorts/Reels/TikTok; thumbnail + title +
  chapters for long YouTube; hook line + scannable body for X/LinkedIn/threads).
  Repurpose by re-cutting to the grammar, never by exporting one file everywhere.

## 3. Hook craft (the first 1–3 seconds decide everything)

Up to ~50% of viewers leave in the first 3s; intro retention is the dominant early
ranking signal. Win this or nothing else matters.

- **Pattern interrupt in frame one** — motion, a striking visual, or high-contrast
  on-screen text. The first frame is a thumbnail.
- **Open a loop / stakes / specificity.** State the payoff or a curiosity gap the
  viewer must stay to close ("I tried X for 30 days — here's what broke"). Specific
  beats clever: a number, a named thing, a concrete claim.
- **Front-load value; cut the throat-clearing.** No "hey guys, welcome back." Start
  in motion. For long-form, deliver a reason-to-stay by ~10s.
- Write 5–10 hook variants per idea; the hook is the highest-leverage edit.

## 4. Series / pillar architecture

- **3–5 content pillars** the channel returns to, each tied to the promise. Pillars
  make ideation infinite and teach the audience what to expect.
- **Repeatable series + formats** (a named recurring segment, a signature structure)
  compound: viewers come back *for the series*, and proven formats de-risk every new
  idea. Vary the topic, keep the container.

## 5. Distribution & repurposing pipeline

- **Atomic-content model:** one deep piece (long video, essay, stream) → many
  platform-native atoms (clips, quotes, threads, carousels), each re-cut to its
  platform's grammar and CTA. One idea, many surfaces.
- Be present where the audience already is; don't spread thin across platforms you
  can't feed natively. Depth on one or two beats shallow omnipresence.

## 6. Retention metrics that matter

Optimize the signals that predict distribution and a real audience, not applause:

- **Hold / intro retention (past 3s)** and **watch-through / average view duration** —
  the primary drivers. A longer piece watched through beats a short one abandoned.
- **Saves and shares** > likes: they signal value worth keeping or passing on.
- **Follower-conversion and returning-viewer rate** — did this turn a viewer into
  an audience member? Vanity likes/views without these don't compound.

## 7. Iterate via post-mortem

After a batch, compare each piece's hook-hold, watch-through, and saves/shares
against your own baseline. Keep what beat baseline, kill what didn't, double the
winning format. Write learnings (and updated baselines) back to `personal/`.

## Honesty boundary

This pack earns attention honestly. No engagement-bait, fake stakes, misleading
thumbnails/titles (clickbait the content doesn't pay off), manufactured outrage, or
deceptive hooks. The hook must be a true promise the content keeps. Audience trust
is the compounding asset; a short-term metric spike that burns it is a net loss.
