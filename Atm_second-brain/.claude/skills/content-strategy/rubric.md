# Content Strategy Rubric (binary — every box must be checked before publishing or shipping a plan)

- [ ] A single specific target viewer is named (not "everyone"), with the recurring promise the content keeps.
- [ ] The positioning has a differentiated angle — something incumbents in the niche can't or won't say — not just a topic.
- [ ] The format fits the idea (transformation/how-to/take/story each in its best container), not forced.
- [ ] The piece is platform-native: cut to the chosen platform's grammar, not raw cross-posted from another.
- [ ] The hook lands in the first 1–3 seconds with a pattern interrupt and a clear reason to stay (loop/stakes/specificity).
- [ ] At least 5 hook variants were written and the strongest chosen; no "hey guys, welcome back" throat-clearing.
- [ ] The first frame works as a standalone thumbnail (motion, striking visual, or high-contrast text).
- [ ] The piece maps to one of 3–5 defined content pillars tied to the promise.
- [ ] It uses a repeatable series or signature format the audience can recognize and return for.
- [ ] A repurposing plan exists: the deep piece is atomized into platform-native cuts, each re-cut (not exported raw).
- [ ] Success is judged on retention metrics that matter (intro hold, watch-through, saves/shares, follower conversion) — not likes/views alone.
- [ ] A post-mortem step compares results against the user's own baseline and feeds the winning format forward.
- [ ] The hook/title/thumbnail is an honest promise the content pays off — no clickbait, engagement-bait, or manufactured stakes.
- [ ] `personal/` recall has been consulted for the user's niche, voice, audience data, and past post performance; any conflict with generic advice is flagged and personal/ (especially proven wins) wins.
