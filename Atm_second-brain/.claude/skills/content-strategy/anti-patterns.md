# Content Strategy Anti-Patterns (detectable failure modes + fix)

## 1. Talking to "everyone"
**Tell:** the audience is described as a broad demographic; the content has no
specific person it's for and no consistent promise.
**Fix:** name one viewer and the recurring promise. Specific content compounds; the
"everyone" channel is for no one and never builds a returning audience.

## 2. Slow open / buried hook
**Tell:** "Hey guys, welcome back to the channel" intros, logo stings, or 10s of
setup before any value; intro retention craters below 40%.
**Fix:** start in motion. Lead with the payoff, stakes, or open loop in the first
1–3s. Cut everything before the hook — it's the highest-leverage edit you have.

## 3. Clickbait the content doesn't pay off
**Tell:** the title/thumbnail/hook promises more than the piece delivers; comments
say "where's the thing in the title."
**Fix:** make the hook a true promise and deliver it. A betrayed promise spikes one
view and burns the trust that drives saves, shares, and returning viewers.

## 4. Raw cross-posting
**Tell:** the identical export (often with another platform's watermark or wrong
aspect ratio) appears on every surface.
**Fix:** repurpose by re-cutting to each platform's native grammar and CTA. Same
idea, different form factor per surface.

## 5. Chasing vanity metrics
**Tell:** decisions are driven by likes/view counts; saves, shares, watch-through,
and follower conversion are never looked at.
**Fix:** judge on retention signals that predict distribution and audience growth.
A high-view, low-watch-through, zero-save post is a flop wearing a costume.

## 6. No format / random one-offs
**Tell:** every upload is a brand-new structure with no recurring series or pillar;
ideation feels like starting from zero each time.
**Fix:** define 3–5 pillars and turn winners into named, repeatable series. The
container compounds and de-risks each new topic.

## 7. Volume without a system (burnout posting)
**Tell:** trying to manually produce many original pieces per platform per week,
leading to thinning quality and burnout.
**Fix:** adopt the atomic model — one deep piece atomized into many native cuts.
Volume should come from repurposing a pillar, not from inventing more ideas.

## 8. Engagement-bait / manufactured stakes
**Tell:** "comment 1 or you lose," fake outrage, rage-bait, or invented drama to
juice the algorithm.
**Fix:** earn engagement with real value and honest curiosity gaps. Baited metrics
attract the wrong audience and decay the trust the channel runs on.
