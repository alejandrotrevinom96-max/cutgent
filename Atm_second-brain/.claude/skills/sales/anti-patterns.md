# Sales — Anti-patterns

- **Pitching before discovery.** Demo in the first minutes. *Fix:* state the buyer's
  specific problem in their words before proposing anything.
- **Feature dumping.** "And it also does..." *Fix:* tie each point to a buyer
  outcome/metric.
- **Happy ears / no qualification.** Forecasting with no economic buyer or process.
  *Fix:* run MEDDIC; a blank field = not a real deal.
- **Single-threading.** The whole deal rides on one contact. *Fix:* map and reach the
  buying group.
- **Steamrolling objections.** Firing a rebuttal instantly. *Fix:* ask what's behind
  it first.
- **Manufactured urgency / pressure (manipulation).** Fake deadlines, invented
  scarcity. *Fix:* only real, buyer-grounded urgency — else remove it (forbidden).
- **Selling a bad fit.** Pushing a deal you know won't solve their problem. *Fix:*
  would they be glad in 6 months? If not, walk away.
- **Discounting before value (negotiating too early).** *Fix:* establish value first;
  hand price/terms to `negotiation` only once both sides want to transact.
- **Vague next steps.** "I'll circle back." *Fix:* specific date, owner, purpose.
- **Talking too much.** Rep dominates. *Fix:* the buyer should talk most in discovery.
