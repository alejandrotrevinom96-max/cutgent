# Sales

A binary, consultative procedure. The goal is to **help the buyer make a good
decision** — including *not* to buy when it doesn't fit. **Whenever `personal/`
recall returns the user's real context — product, ICP, this account's history,
motion, pricing, prior calls — that OVERRIDES the generic moves below.**

**Honesty is non-negotiable.** No manipulation, no manufactured pressure, no selling
what doesn't fit. Those tactics win one deal and lose the renewal, the referral, and
your reputation.

## 1. Research & target
Confirm ICP fit; learn their business, likely problem, recent triggers, and the
players. A tailored opening earns the meeting; a generic blast wastes both sides' time.

## 2. Discovery FIRST — diagnose before you prescribe
Understand the problem before proposing the solution. Use **SPIN** (Situation
sparingly, Problem, Implication, Need-payoff — Implication and Need-payoff move
large deals by making the buyer quantify their own pain). Use **Gap Selling**:
current state -> desired future state -> the gap; the size of the gap is the value.
No gap -> no deal, and that's fine. **Listen more than you talk.**

## 3. Qualify (MEDDIC / BANT-style)
Metrics (quantified outcome), Economic buyer (who controls budget), Decision criteria
& process, Identify pain (reason to act now), Champion (internal advocate). If you
can't fill these in, you have a conversation, not a forecast-able deal.

## 4. Sell the outcome, not features
Tie everything to the buyer's own metric from discovery. Features are evidence; value
is the changed business outcome in their terms. Challenger: **teach** an insight,
**tailor** it, **take control** of the process (constructively, never pressuring).

## 5. Quantify the cost of inaction
The real competitor is "do nothing." Make the status quo's cost explicit in *their*
numbers — honest framing, not invented urgency.

## 6. Handle objections by understanding them
An objection is information. Diagnose before responding ("Help me understand the
concern"). Most objections are unmet needs, unaddressed risks, or missing
stakeholders — not "no." If it reveals a genuine misfit, say so.

## 7. Multithread
Single-threaded deals die. Map all stakeholders (economic buyer, champion, users,
blockers, legal/procurement) and build more than one relationship.

## 8. Clear next step every call — and ask for the close
Every interaction ends with a specific, scheduled next step. When discovery, value,
and qualification are in place, explicitly ask for the decision — respectful clarity,
not pressure.

## 9. Honest fit — walk away when it doesn't fit
If the product won't solve their problem, say so and walk away. This protects the
buyer, your retention, and your referrals.

## Relationship to the negotiation pack
Selling precedes negotiating. This pack gets you to a qualified, value-established,
willing deal. Once the conversation turns to terms and price (anchoring, concessions,
BATNA/ZOPA, trades), hand off to `negotiation`. Don't discount an unsold deal.
