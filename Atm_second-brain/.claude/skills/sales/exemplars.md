# Sales — Exemplars

## Neil Rackham — *SPIN Selling*
From research on thousands of calls: closing tricks that work in small sales hurt in
large ones. What works in big deals is question-led discovery — Situation (sparingly),
Problem, Implication (consequences/cost), Need-payoff (buyer states the value). **Why:**
the buyer talks themselves into the value; you diagnose instead of push.

## Dixon & Adamson — *The Challenger Sale*
Top performers Teach-Tailor-Take control: teach the customer something new about their
business, tailor it to the stakeholder, take control of the process with constructive
assertiveness. **Why:** buyers value insight over "what keeps you up at night?"
**Caution:** take control != steamroll; teach a real insight, not manufactured fear.

## Keenan — *Gap Selling*
The deal is the gap between current and desired future state. Diagnose the current
state (impact + root cause), define the future state; the size/cost of the gap is the
value and urgency. **Why:** forces real diagnosis and surfaces honest "no gap -> no deal."

## Mike Weinberg — *New Sales Simplified*
Tight target list, a buyer-focused sales story (problems you solve, not features),
proactive outbound. **Why:** counters passivity; keeps the message about the customer.

## Consultative discovery done well
Researched opening referencing a specific problem; buyer talks ~70%; implication/gap
questions get the buyer to quantify pain; rep confirms understanding before any
solution talk; economic buyer + process named; call ends with a scheduled next step;
if it's not a fit, the rep says so and exits — and is remembered well for it.
