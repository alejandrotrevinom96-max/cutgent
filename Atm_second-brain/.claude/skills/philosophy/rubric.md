# Philosophy rubric (BINARY)

Pass requires every applicable box checked. Skip a box only if it genuinely does
not apply (e.g. no ethical content), and say why.

- [ ] The question is classified as empirical, conceptual, or normative (or a named mix).
- [ ] Premises and the conclusion are stated explicitly and separately.
- [ ] Validity is assessed (does the conclusion follow from the premises?) on its own.
- [ ] Soundness is assessed separately (are the premises actually true?).
- [ ] At least one hidden/unstated assumption is surfaced and named.
- [ ] No is-ought gap: any "should" is grounded in a stated value premise, not derived from facts alone.
- [ ] The strongest opposing view is steelmanned (stated as its best defender would).
- [ ] For a moral question, at least THREE ethical lenses are applied and compared (not one picked).
- [ ] Where the lenses agree vs diverge is made explicit.
- [ ] Beliefs are held provisionally; what would change the conclusion is stated (fallibilism).
- [ ] The response checks for motivated reasoning / wanting a particular answer.
- [ ] No fallacy is committed or relied on (false dilemma, strawman, ad hominem, equivocation, appeal to authority, etc.).
- [ ] Any wisdom tradition invoked is applied to the actual situation, not quoted as doctrine.
- [ ] Technical terms (valid, sound, deontology, a priori, etc.) are defined in plain language.
- [ ] The conclusion is tentative, with its weakest point named — not dogmatic.
- [ ] Validity is not conflated with truth (form alone does not make a conclusion true).
- [ ] The user's `personal/` values/context were consulted and lead; generic best practice does not override them.
