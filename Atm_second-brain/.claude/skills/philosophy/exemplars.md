# Philosophy exemplars

Borrow the *mechanism*, not the conclusion.

## Socratic method (elenchus) — examine, don't assert
Socrates asks rather than lectures: elicit a definition ("justice is giving each
what is owed"), find a counterexample (return a weapon to a madman?), force
refinement. **Mechanism:** test a belief by drawing out its consequences until a
contradiction surfaces, then revise. Use to examine the user's own claim — to
clarify, not to "win." The payoff is productive puzzlement, not a gotcha.

## Steelmanning vs strawmanning
Strawman: attack a caricature. Steelman: state the opponent's view so well they'd
say "yes, exactly," *then* engage. **Mechanism:** reconstruct the best, repaired
version first; if your objection still lands against the steel version, it's real
(Rapoport's rule).

## Trolley problem — to CONTRAST lenses, not "solve"
Divert a trolley to kill one instead of five? Push a man off a bridge for the same
effect? **Mechanism:** outcome is held fixed (1 vs 5) to isolate intuitions.
Consequentialism: save five in both. Deontology balks at *using* the man as a means.
Virtue/care ask what kind of agent does this. The lesson is *why principled people
diverge* — that's where the moral structure lives.

## Rawls' veil of ignorance — a procedure for fairness
Choose society's rules not knowing who you'll be in it. **Mechanism:** strip
self-interest by removing knowledge of your position; principles chosen behind the
veil are fair because no one can game them. Apply to any "is this fair?": would you
accept this rule not knowing which side you'd land on?

## Stoic dichotomy of control — applied, not quoted
Some things are up to us (judgments, choices, effort), some are not (outcomes,
others, the past). **Mechanism:** sort a worry into the two buckets; invest only in
the first, accept the second. Not fatalism — a focusing tool. Use on the actual
situation.

## Popper's falsifiability / fallibilism — what would prove you wrong?
A claim no possible evidence could refute isn't strong, it's empty. **Mechanism:**
demand that a belief specify what observation would count *against* it; hold every
belief open to revision. "What would change my mind?" — if the honest answer is
"nothing," that's a flag, not a virtue.
