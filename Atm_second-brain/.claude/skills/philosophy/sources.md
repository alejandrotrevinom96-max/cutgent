# Philosophy sources

Primary texts are durable — read them directly. Use the Stanford Encyclopedia of
Philosophy (SEP) as the living reference for current scholarship and definitions.

## Primary / canonical
- **Plato**, *Dialogues* — *Apology*, *Euthyphro*, *Republic*, *Meno* (Socratic
  method, the examined life, justice).
- **Aristotle**, *Nicomachean Ethics* — virtue, the mean, eudaimonia, habituation.
- **Stoics**: **Epictetus** (*Enchiridion*, *Discourses*), **Marcus Aurelius**
  (*Meditations*), **Seneca** (*Letters from a Stoic*).
- **Kant**, *Groundwork of the Metaphysics of Morals* — duty, categorical
  imperative, persons as ends.
- **Mill**, *Utilitarianism* (and *On Liberty*) — consequentialism.
- **Rawls**, *A Theory of Justice* — original position, veil of ignorance. (Scanlon,
  *What We Owe to Each Other* — contractualism.)
- **Popper**, *Conjectures and Refutations* — falsifiability, fallibilism.
- **Existentialism**: **Sartre**, *Existentialism Is a Humanism*; **Camus**, *The
  Myth of Sisyphus*.
- **Pragmatism**: **William James**, *Pragmatism*; **Dewey**.
- **Care ethics**: **Gilligan**, *In a Different Voice*; **Noddings**.

## Good introductions
- **Thomas Nagel**, *What Does It All Mean?* — short, clear tour of core problems.
- **Simon Blackburn**, *Think* — reasoning, knowledge, free will, ethics for beginners.
- **Michael Sandel**, *Justice* — the major ethical lenses on live cases.
- **William B. Irvine**, *A Guide to the Good Life* — practical Stoicism.

## Living reference
- **Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy (SEP)** — peer-reviewed, current; the
  default for any term/thinker/debate. (Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy for a
  second reading.) Primary texts state positions durably; SEP tracks where the
  debate stands.
