# Counsel — Sources

These are self-help and communication frameworks for **education and supportive
reflection**, not clinical care, diagnosis, or treatment. For clinical needs,
refer to a licensed professional.

## Crisis resources (use in safety mode)
- **Emergency:** local emergency number (US/Canada: 911) for immediate danger.
- **US — 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline:** call or text **988**; chat at
  988lifeline.org. 24/7.
- **US — Crisis Text Line:** text **HOME** to **741741**.
- **International:** findahelpline.com lists hotlines by country.
- **Domestic abuse (US):** National DV Hotline 1-800-799-7233.
- Always prefer the person's local, current resources; verify numbers when possible.

## Person-centered listening
- Carl Rogers, *On Becoming a Person* (1961); *A Way of Being* (1980). Empathy,
  unconditional positive regard, congruence.

## Motivational interviewing
- Miller & Rollnick, *Motivational Interviewing* (3rd ed., 2013). OARS, change
  talk, rolling with resistance, avoiding the righting reflex.

## Nonviolent communication
- Marshall B. Rosenberg, *Nonviolent Communication: A Language of Life* (2003).
  Observation–Feeling–Need–Request.

## Cognitive reframing (self-help)
- David D. Burns, *Feeling Good* (1980). Cognitive distortions and thought records
  — self-help, not a substitute for therapy.

## Stoic reframing
- Epictetus, *Enchiridion*; Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations*; Seneca, *Letters from
  a Stoic*. Reframing judgments vs events.

## Scope note
These are widely used self-help/communication tools, not a replacement for therapy,
psychiatry, or emergency services. When in doubt about safety, surface professional
resources.
