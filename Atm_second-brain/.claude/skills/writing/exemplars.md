# Exemplars — Writing

The personal layer (the user's own voice) overrides.

## William Zinsser — *On Writing Well*
Clarity, simplicity, brevity, humanity; editing as removing clutter. **Why:** every
needless word/qualifier/jargon weakens the sentence; "humanity" keeps clarity from
going sterile. The default ethos for explanatory prose.

## Strunk & White — *The Elements of Style* (use with critique)
"Omit needless words"; concision, active voice, concrete language. **Useful** as
discipline. **Critique:** it overreaches and is sometimes wrong (its passive-voice
advice is muddled; several "rules" are taste presented as law — see Pullum). Take the
instinct, not the dogma.

## Verlyn Klinkenborg — *Several Short Sentences About Writing*
The sentence is the unit of composition; build one clear, well-made sentence at a
time; vary length deliberately. **Why:** shifts attention from vague "flow" to the
concrete craft where rhythm, clarity, and force are decided.

## Anne Lamott — *Bird by Bird*
Permission for the "shitty first draft"; one piece at a time. **Why:** defeats
perfectionism and separates generating from editing — doing both at once stalls writers.

## Steven Pinker — *The Sense of Style*
"Classic style" (prose as a window onto something real) and the **curse of knowledge**
(you forget the reader doesn't know what you know). **Why:** explains *why* good prose
works (the reader's mental model) and corrects S&W's errors with linguistics.

## John McPhee — *Draft No. 4*
Structure/spine and the long grind of revision in narrative nonfiction; the structure
is found before and during drafting. **Why:** treats architecture as a first-class
problem and normalizes heavy, iterative revision.
