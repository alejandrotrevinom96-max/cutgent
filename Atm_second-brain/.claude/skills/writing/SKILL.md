# Writing

A method for clear, compelling long-form nonfiction, judged against the binary
rubric — not a "you are a great writer" persona. Scope: essays, articles,
explanatory and narrative prose, long docs. NOT conversion copy (see `copywriting`).

## PRECEDENCE: personal layer wins
The user's `personal/` layer (voice/register, target audience, formats, words they
avoid, examples of their writing they like), via recall, **OVERRIDES the generic
defaults here.** Match their voice; say so when you apply it.

## Method
1. **One controlling idea.** The single thing the piece is about, statable in one
   sentence. Everything serves it; cut what doesn't.
2. **Know the reader + purpose.** Who's reading, what they know, what you want them
   to think/feel/do. Beware the **curse of knowledge** (Pinker).
3. **Structure before prose.** Build the throughline/outline (or narrative arc)
   first. Find the *spine* (McPhee). Prose written before structure gets thrown out.
4. **Bad first draft, then revise hard.** Permission for a "shitty first draft"
   (Lamott) — then **writing is rewriting.** The draft is raw material.
5. **Concrete and specific over abstract.** Names, numbers, images, examples — not
   vague abstraction.
6. **Cut ruthlessly.** "Omit needless words." Most first drafts lose a third with no
   loss of meaning — usually a gain.
7. **Strong verbs, active voice.** Prefer active voice and vivid verbs over
   nominalizations and "to be"; cut adverbs propping up weak verbs.
8. **Vary sentence rhythm.** Mix short and long. A short sentence lands. Monotone
   length deadens prose (Klinkenborg: the sentence is the unit of composition).
9. **Show, don't (only) tell.** Dramatize and evidence claims; let the reader conclude.
10. **Read it aloud.** The ear catches clunks, run-ons, and false notes. Always.

## Revision passes (order)
Structure (does the throughline hold?) -> cut (remove needless words/sections) ->
sentence-level (verbs, voice, rhythm) -> line edit (word choice) -> read aloud ->
proofread.

## Honesty rules
- **No padding to hit a length; no fabrication** (facts, quotes, statistics). Honesty
  ("humanity," Zinsser) over impressive-sounding emptiness.
- Treat Strunk & White as useful discipline, **not scripture** (its passive-voice and
  "which/that" rules are oversimplified — apply judgment).
- Clarity over cleverness; clever that obscures is a failure.
