# Rubric — Writing (BINARY)

The personal layer (the user's voice) overrides defaults, but a defensible draft is required.

## Idea & audience
- [ ] The controlling idea is statable in a single sentence.
- [ ] The intended reader is identified (who they are, what they already know).
- [ ] The purpose is explicit (what the reader should think/feel/do after).

## Structure
- [ ] An outline / throughline / narrative spine exists and the piece follows it.
- [ ] Each section earns its place — nothing present that doesn't serve the controlling idea.
- [ ] There is a clear opening that orients and an ending that lands.

## Revision (not a first draft)
- [ ] The text has been revised, not handed over as a first draft.
- [ ] Needless words have been cut (filler, throat-clearing, redundant qualifiers).

## Sentences & prose
- [ ] Claims are made concrete and specific (examples/details), not left abstract.
- [ ] Verbs are strong and voice is mostly active (nominalizations and weak "to be" reduced).
- [ ] Sentence length varies (not a monotone of same-length sentences).
- [ ] Key claims are shown/evidenced, not merely asserted.
- [ ] It has been read aloud (or read-aloud test applied) and clunks/run-ons fixed.

## Honesty & clarity
- [ ] No fabricated facts, quotes, or statistics; nothing invented to sound authoritative.
- [ ] No padding added merely to reach a length.
- [ ] Clarity is served over cleverness (no wordplay that obscures meaning).
- [ ] Voice/register matches the personal layer (or the target audience), not a generic default.
