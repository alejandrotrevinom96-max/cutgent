# Sources — Business Strategy

Frameworks are tools, not gospel; the personal layer overrides generic best practice.

- **Michael E. Porter** — *Competitive Strategy* (1980); *Competitive Advantage*
  (1985); "What Is Strategy?" (HBR, 1996). Five forces, generic strategies,
  positioning, trade-offs.
- **Clayton M. Christensen** — *The Innovator's Dilemma* (1997); *Competing
  Against Luck* (2016). Disruption and Jobs To Be Done.
- **Peter Thiel** — *Zero to One* (2014). Monopoly vs competition, durable
  advantages, last-mover advantage.
- **Eric Ries** — *The Lean Startup* (2011). Build-Measure-Learn, MVP, validated
  learning, pivot/persevere.
- **Geoffrey A. Moore** — *Crossing the Chasm* (1991; rev. 2014). Adoption
  lifecycle, beachhead, whole product.
- **Peter F. Drucker** — *The Practice of Management* (1954). "The purpose of a
  business is to create a customer"; marketing and innovation.
- **W. Chan Kim & Renée Mauborgne** — *Blue Ocean Strategy* (2005). Value
  innovation, ERRC grid, strategy canvas.
- **Hamilton Helmer** — *7 Powers* (2016). A taxonomy of moats.
- **Bryar & Carr** — *Working Backwards* (2021). Amazon's PR/FAQ method.

## How to weigh them
Use multiple lenses; prefer evidence from your own customers; mark anything that
depends on market/stage/funding as context-dependent — the personal layer decides.
