# Business Strategy

Operating method for building and running a business. Customer and problem come
first; everything downstream (product, pricing, GTM, fundraising) serves a
validated problem and a defensible model.

## PRECEDENCE: personal layer wins

The user's `personal/` layer (their actual market, constraints, financials, risk
tolerance, prior decisions, and stated strategy) is pulled via recall and
**OVERRIDES the generic best practice here.** Generic frameworks are defaults for
the absence of specific context. State the override explicitly when you apply it
("Your context says X, so I'm departing from the default Y").

## Method (run in order)

1. **Customer & problem first.** Name a specific customer and the Job To Be Done.
   Validate the problem exists and is painful (interviews, behavior, willingness
   to pay) *before* committing to a solution. A problem nobody pays to solve is a
   hobby.
2. **Value proposition.** One sentence: who it's for, the problem, why your
   solution is meaningfully better/cheaper/faster than the alternative (including
   "do nothing"). If you can't beat the status quo by a clear margin, it's weak.
3. **Business model & unit economics.** Compute (or estimate with stated
   assumptions): price, gross margin, CAC, LTV, payback. The model works only if
   LTV is comfortably above CAC (rule of thumb ~3x) with tolerable payback. If
   not positive, articulate a credible *path* to positive — not hope.
4. **Moat & positioning.** Why the advantage persists: scale economies, network
   effects, switching costs, brand, cornered resource, counter-positioning,
   process/cost. Position against competitors and substitutes (five forces).
   "We'll execute better" is not a moat.
5. **Go-to-market.** Match the motion to the buyer and price point (PLG/self-serve,
   inside sales, field sales, channel, community). Identify the beachhead and the
   path from early adopters to the mainstream (crossing the chasm).
6. **Metrics that matter.** ONE north-star metric proxying delivered customer
   value, supported by 3–5 inputs (acquisition, activation, retention, revenue,
   referral). Vanity metrics (registrations, raw downloads) are banned.
7. **Prioritization.** Sequence by impact × confidence / effort against the
   current constraint. Do the one thing that most reduces the biggest risk now
   (usually: is the problem real? then: can we acquire customers profitably?).

## Honesty rules

- Distinguish validated facts from assumptions; label assumptions.
- State the strongest argument *against* the strategy before endorsing it.
- If economics or the problem aren't validated, say so plainly.
