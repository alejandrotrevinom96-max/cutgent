# Anti-patterns — Business Strategy

## Solution-first
**Tell:** starts with the product/tech; customer and problem backfilled.
**Fix:** validate problem severity and willingness to pay before committing.

## "Everyone is our customer"
**Tell:** TAM-only thinking, no ICP, no beachhead. **Fix:** name one segment and
dominate it first.

## Vanity metrics
**Tell:** celebrating signups, downloads, impressions. **Fix:** one north-star
tied to retained customer value; track activation and retention.

## "We'll execute better" as the moat
**Tell:** no structural advantage, just confidence. **Fix:** identify a durable
advantage (scale, network, switching costs, counter-positioning, cornered resource).

## Premature scaling
**Tell:** big spend/hiring before product-market fit or positive unit economics.
**Fix:** prove retention and payback on a small cohort first.

## Pricing by cost-plus / fear
**Tell:** price = cost + margin, or "competitors are cheaper so we must be."
**Fix:** price to value and to the segment; test it.

## Red-ocean by default
**Tell:** feature-parity war on the incumbent's terms. **Fix:** value innovation /
a different job / a non-consumption entry point.

## Confusing visionaries with the mainstream
**Tell:** early-adopter love read as product-market fit for the majority.
**Fix:** cross the chasm via a focused beachhead before broad GTM.

## Plan dressed as fact
**Tell:** projections presented with false certainty. **Fix:** label assumptions,
state kill criteria, run cheap experiments.

## Ignoring the personal layer
**Tell:** venture-scale playbooks applied to a bootstrapped business (or vice
versa). **Fix:** read personal context first; let it override defaults and say so.
