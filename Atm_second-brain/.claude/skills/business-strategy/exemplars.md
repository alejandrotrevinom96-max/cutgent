# Exemplars — Business Strategy

Canonical reasoning patterns; templates for analysis, not slogans.

## 1. Jobs To Be Done / Disruption (Christensen)
Customers "hire" a product to make progress in a circumstance. Disruption starts
low-end or in non-consumption with a "good enough," cheaper offering, then moves
upmarket. **Milkshake:** commuters "hired" a milkshake to make a boring drive
interesting — the competitor was a bagel, not other shakes. **Use:** define the
problem by job, spot entry points incumbents ignore.

## 2. Five Forces / Positioning (Porter)
Industry profit is shaped by rivalry, new entrants, substitutes, buyer power,
supplier power. Strategy = a deliberate, hard-to-copy position with explicit
trade-offs about what you will NOT do. **Southwest:** point-to-point, one aircraft
type, no meals/assigned seats — a coherent activity system rivals couldn't copy.
The "no" choices made the moat.

## 3. Blue Ocean
Create uncontested space (raise/create buyer value while reducing/eliminating cost
drivers). **Cirque du Soleil:** dropped animals/stars (cost), raised artistry,
created a theatrical theme — a new market between circus and theatre. Use the
Eliminate-Reduce-Raise-Create grid.

## 4. Amazon Working Backwards
Start from the customer outcome: write the press release and FAQ *before* building.
If the PR isn't compelling, don't build. Forces clarity on the benefit and the bar.

## 5. Lean Startup (Ries)
Treat the business as hypotheses. Build-Measure-Learn with an MVP; pursue validated
learning; pivot or persevere on evidence. **Dropbox** tested demand with an
explainer video before building sync.

## 6. Crossing the Chasm (Moore)
Visionaries and the pragmatist early majority buy differently. Cross by dominating
a single beachhead niche with a whole product, then expand.

## 7. Unit economics (worked numbers)
Price $50/mo, 80% margin → $40 contribution. Retention 24 mo → LTV ≈ $960. CAC
$300 → LTV/CAC ≈ 3.2 (healthy); payback ≈ 7.5 mo. Viable if cash can fund a
7.5-mo payback at the desired growth rate; biggest upside lever here is retention.
