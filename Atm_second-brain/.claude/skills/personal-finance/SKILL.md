# Personal Finance

A method for ordering personal money decisions so the high-leverage moves happen
first. Built on low-cost, diversified, behavior-aware principles.

## DISCLAIMER (state to the user)

This is **financial education, not individualized financial, tax, legal, or
investment advice**, and not a recommendation to buy or sell any security. No
fiduciary relationship is created. Specifics depend on your situation and your
jurisdiction's tax law. For large or irreversible decisions, consult a licensed
fee-only (fiduciary) advisor, a tax professional, and/or an attorney. Past
performance does not guarantee future results; investing involves risk of loss.

## PRECEDENCE: personal layer wins

The user's `personal/` layer (income, debts, balances, goals, risk tolerance, tax

## Order of operations (general default)

1. **Cashflow / budget.** Spend less than you earn; create a deliberate saving
   gap. The saving rate is the biggest lever for most people.
2. **Starter emergency fund.** ~1 month of essential expenses in cash.
3. **Capture the employer match.** Contribute enough to get any workplace-plan
   match fully — an immediate ~100% return (plan/jurisdiction dependent).
4. **Kill high-interest debt.** Aggressively pay debt above ~6–8% APR. Clearing a
   22% card is a guaranteed 22% return.
5. **Full emergency fund.** ~3–6 months of essential expenses (more if income is
   volatile or single-earner; less if very stable — personal layer decides).
6. **Tax-advantaged investing.** Use available tax-advantaged accounts before
   taxable brokerage.
7. **Invest the rest, low-cost & diversified.** Broad, low-fee index funds matched
   to horizon and risk tolerance; automate contributions.
8. **Insure against catastrophe.** Health, liability, and (if others depend on
   your income) term life and disability. Insure the rare and ruinous; self-insure
   the small.
9. **Goal-based planning.** Tie money to written goals with timelines; size and
   locate assets by when you'll need them.

## Core investing principles (Bogle)

- Minimize costs (expense ratios, advisory fees, taxes, turnover) — low cost is the
  most reliable predictor of relative returns.
- Diversify broadly (total-market index) over stock-picking or timing.
- Match stock/bond mix to horizon and risk; rebalance; stay the course.

## Honesty rules

- Separate near-certain principles (low cost, diversification, spend less than you
  earn) from debated/jurisdiction-dependent rules of thumb (4% rule, exact
  emergency-fund size, account types).
- Never imply guaranteed returns. Flag where a licensed professional is warranted.
