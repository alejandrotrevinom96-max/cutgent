# Rubric — Personal Finance (BINARY)

Pass/fail; check only if objectively true for this person. "Jurisdiction" boxes
require the locally correct vehicle. The personal layer overrides defaults, but a
defensible answer is still required. Education, not advice.

## Cashflow
- [ ] Monthly income and expenses are known (tracked, not guessed).
- [ ] Spending is less than income — there is a positive saving gap.
- [ ] A target saving rate (percent of income) is written down.

## Safety net
- [ ] A starter cash buffer (~1 month essentials) exists or is the immediate next step.
- [ ] A full emergency fund target (e.g. 3–6 months, adjusted for income stability) is set.

## Debt
- [ ] All debts are listed with balance and interest rate (APR).
- [ ] A specific payoff plan exists for high-interest debt (above ~6–8% APR), with a chosen method and rationale.
- [ ] Any employer retirement match is captured before extra paydown of low-rate debt (where a match exists).

## Investing
- [ ] Available tax-advantaged accounts are used before taxable investing (per jurisdiction).
- [ ] The portfolio is diversified and broad (e.g. total-market index funds), not concentrated in single stocks.
- [ ] Investment costs are low (expense ratios / advisory fees identified and minimized).
- [ ] Stock/bond allocation is matched to the goal horizon and stated risk tolerance.
- [ ] Contributions are automated and there is a stay-the-course plan (no market timing).

## Risk / insurance
- [ ] Catastrophic risks are insured (health, liability; term life and disability if others depend on the income).
- [ ] Small, non-ruinous risks are NOT over-insured.

## Goals & honesty
- [ ] At least one financial goal is written with an amount and a timeline.
- [ ] No assumption of guaranteed or specific market returns is being made.
- [ ] Jurisdiction-dependent items are flagged, and professional help is noted where warranted.
