# Anti-patterns — Personal Finance

Costly, common mistakes. Education, not advice.

## Investing before paying off high-interest debt
**Tell:** buying index funds while carrying a 20%+ card balance. **Fix:** capture
any match, then clear high-interest debt — a guaranteed return beats an uncertain one.

## No emergency fund → debt spiral
**Tell:** no cash buffer; every surprise goes on a card. **Fix:** build a starter
buffer before aggressive investing.

## Stock-picking / market timing / chasing performance
**Tell:** picking single stocks, jumping in/out, buying last year's winners or hot
tips. **Fix:** broad low-cost index, automated, held through volatility.

## Paying high fees
**Tell:** 1%+ AUM advisor, high expense-ratio or load funds, frequent trading.
**Fix:** low-cost index funds; if you use an advisor, choose fee-only fiduciary.

## Treating the 4% rule (or "12% returns") as a guarantee
**Tell:** planning around fixed high returns or a rigid 4% withdrawal as certain.
**Fix:** conservative estimates, flexible withdrawals, labeled assumptions.

## Lifestyle inflation
**Tell:** spending rises with every raise; saving rate flat. **Fix:** bank raises;
automate increased contributions.

## Under- or over-insuring
**Tell:** no health/disability/liability cover, OR whole-life + extended warranties
on cheap goods. **Fix:** insure the rare and ruinous; self-insure the small.

## Cash drag / long-term money in savings
**Tell:** decades-horizon money entirely in cash "to be safe." **Fix:** match the
vehicle to the horizon; long-term money is invested, near-term money is safe.

## Tax-inefficiency / leaving the match on the table
**Tell:** skipping the match; taxable before tax-advantaged. **Fix:** capture the
match; use tax-advantaged space first (per jurisdiction).

## Applying US-specific advice abroad
**Tell:** recommending US account types to someone elsewhere. **Fix:** use
principles, flag jurisdiction, defer specifics to local rules; let the personal
layer (which states the jurisdiction) override.
