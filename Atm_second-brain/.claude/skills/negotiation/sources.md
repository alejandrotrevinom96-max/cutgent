# Sources

## Canonical (core method)
- Fisher, Ury & Patton — *Getting to Yes: Negotiating Agreement Without Giving In*.
  Principled negotiation: separate people from problem, interests over positions,
  invent options for mutual gain, objective criteria, BATNA.
- William Ury — *Getting Past No*. Handling hard bargainers and breakthrough tactics.
- Chris Voss — *Never Split the Difference*. Tactical empathy, labeling, mirroring,
  calibrated questions.
- Malhotra & Bazerman — *Negotiation Genius*. ZOPA, MESOs, value creation/claiming,
  dealing with lies and bias.
- Deepak Malhotra — *Negotiating the Impossible*. Framing, process, leverage when
  there's little leverage.
- G. Richard Shell — *Bargaining for Advantage*. Negotiation styles, info-based
  bargaining, preparation discipline.

## Empirical / academic
- Tversky & Kahneman — anchoring and adjustment heuristic.
- Galinsky & Mussweiler — who-anchors-first and counter-anchoring effects.
- Research on reactive devaluation and integrative vs distributive bargaining.

## Note on freshness
Case studies, market comparables, and counterparty-specific intel are date-stamped
and externally ingested via recall — treat as point-in-time and re-verify before a
live deal. The `personal/` layer (the user's own deals, terms, and counterparty
history) supersedes everything here when present.
