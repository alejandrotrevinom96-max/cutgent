# Negotiation

A binary, live-deal procedure. Generic best practice is the floor. **Whenever
`personal/` recall returns the user's real deal context — this counterparty's
history, the user's constraints, prior agreed terms, walk-away limits — that
OVERRIDES the generic moves below.** Surface the conflict, then follow personal/.

## 1. Prep (before any contact)

Run the rubric. Don't enter a live exchange until these are concrete numbers/words:

- **Interests, not positions.** For each side list the *why* under the *what*.
  Positions conflict; interests often don't. Trade on differently-valued issues.
- **BATNA** = your best alternative if this deal dies. Make it specific and, if
  weak, improve it *before* negotiating. Your BATNA sets your real reservation point.
- **Reservation point** (walk-away) and **target** (ambitious-but-justifiable).
  Estimate the counterparty's too. The overlap is the **ZOPA**. No ZOPA → don't
  negotiate price; change the issue set or walk.
- **Anchor.** Decide whether to open. Open first when you have decent information;
  let them open when you're uncertain. Anchor at the edge of the *defensible* range
  with a rationale attached. A weak/unjustified anchor is worse than none.
- **Concession plan.** Pre-script each concession *with what you ask in return*.
  Never give a unilateral concession. Decreasing concession sizes signal the floor.
- **Process.** Who speaks, who decides, agenda, package vs sequential, deadline
  reality, authority limits on both sides.

## 2. In the room

- **Ask before telling.** Calibrated open questions ("How am I supposed to…?",
  "What's driving the timeline?") surface interests and constraints cheaply.
- **Label + mirror (tactical empathy).** Name the other side's emotion/position
  ("It sounds like budget is the real constraint"). Lowers temperature, gets data,
  costs nothing. This is reading them honestly — not deceiving them.
- **Trade, don't concede.** "If X, then Y." Make multi-issue offers so value is
  created on issues each side weighs differently. Use MESOs (several equivalent
  offers at once) to learn preferences without weakening your position.
- **Defend the anchor; re-anchor on facts, not splitting.** Counter an extreme
  anchor by naming it and resetting to your rationale — don't negotiate from their
  number.
- **Silence and patience.** After you state a number or ask, stop talking.

## 3. Live cockpit — surface in real time

- Current offer vs **target / reservation / ZOPA** (still inside it?).
- Next pre-planned concession and its **trade-for**.
- The unanswered calibrated question to ask next.
- Any term conflicting with `personal/` constraints or prior agreements — flag loudly.
- A standing reminder of the **BATNA** so the user can walk without flinching.

## 4. Close

Summarize agreed terms aloud, get explicit confirmation, lock who-does-what-by-when,
confirm the counterparty has **authority** to commit. Get it in writing.

## 5. Post-mortem

Record final terms vs target/reservation, which concessions were traded vs given,
their actual interests/BATNA, and what to do differently. Write learnings back to
`personal/`.

## Honesty boundary

This pack sharpens *your* strategy, framing, and responses. It will not fabricate
facts, invent fake alternatives, or script deception against a counterparty. Strong
negotiation is information + preparation + discipline, not manipulation.
