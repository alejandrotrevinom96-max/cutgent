# Communication — Exemplars

Worked structures. Adapt; don't copy.

## 1. The one-sentence core message
Force the whole talk into one sentence the audience could repeat at dinner.
- Weak (topic): "My talk is about our Q3 results."
- Strong (message): "We should double down on SMB because it's growing 3x faster at half the cost."
Test: if a slide doesn't serve this sentence, cut it.

## 2. Audience-and-goal brief (fill before writing)
- Audience: who, what they know, what they care about, their objections.
- Goal: after this, they will **think / feel / do** ___.
- The one sentence they should remember: ___.

## 3. Pixar story spine
> Once upon a time ___. Every day ___. Until one day ___. Because of that ___.
> Because of that ___. Until finally ___. And ever since ___.
Use for case studies, founder stories, change narratives. The "until one day" is
your tension — don't skip it.

## 4. Hook / tension / resolution
- **Hook (first 30s):** a surprising fact, vivid story, sharp question, or stake.
- **Tension:** the gap — a problem, mystery, or cost of inaction.
- **Resolution:** your idea closes the gap; end on the action you want.
Example open: "Last year we lost our biggest customer in a single email. Here's
what that email taught us about churn — and the fix that saved the next ten."

## 5. Minto Pyramid (top-down, for execs)
Answer first, then grouped supporting arguments, then evidence.
> **Recommendation:** Enter the EU market in Q1. Because (1) demand is proven,
> (2) we have a distribution partner, (3) cost is recoverable in 9 months.
Use when the audience wants the bottom line up front (BLUF).

## 6. Made to Stick (SUCCES)
Simple · Unexpected · Concrete · Credible · Emotional · Story.
> Abstract: "Our product saves time."
> Sticky: "Maria used to spend every Friday on payroll. Now it's done before her coffee's cold."

## 7. Signposting language
Preview: "There are three things you need to know." Transition: "That's the
problem. Here's what we did." Recap: "So: faster, cheaper, proven. One ask…"

## 8. Slide minimalism (Duarte / Reynolds)
One idea per slide; if it has two, split it. Replace bullet walls with one image +
a few words, or one big number. Slides are billboards (glanceable in 3s), not
documents. You are the presentation; slides are the backdrop.

## 9. Delivery
Rehearse aloud, standing, timed (3+ run-throughs). Slow down on the key line;
**pause** before and after it. Cut filler ("um," "basically"). Eye contact in
segments; breathe; land the last word of each sentence.

## 10. Q&A
List the 5 questions you hope nobody asks; prepare honest answers. Bridge: answer,
then connect back to the core message. "I don't know" + "here's how I'll find out"
is credible; bluffing isn't. Restate hostile questions neutrally before answering.

## 11. Difficult conversation
> "I want us to ship this well together *(shared goal)*. In the last two reviews
> the feedback came in after the deadline *(observation, not 'you're always
> late')*. That puts the release at risk and I get stressed *(impact)*. Can we
> agree feedback lands by Wednesday? *(specific ask)* — What's getting in the way
> on your end? *(then listen)*"
