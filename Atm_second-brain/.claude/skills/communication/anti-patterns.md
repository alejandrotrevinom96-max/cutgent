# Communication — Anti-Patterns

## The knowledge dump
Telling everything you know; the audience drowns and remembers nothing. **Fix:**
one core message; cut everything that doesn't serve it.

## No clear goal
Presenting without knowing what you want the audience to do. **Fix:** define
think/feel/do before writing a word.

## Burying the lede
Building to the point over 20 slides for an audience that wanted the answer first.
**Fix:** for decisions, use Minto/BLUF — answer up front.

## Weak open
"Hi, my name is… today I'm going to talk about…" loses them in 30s. **Fix:** hook
with a story, stat, question, or stake.

## No tension
All resolution, no problem. Without a gap there's no reason to listen. **Fix:**
establish stakes/cost/mystery before the answer.

## Slides as teleprompter
Reading dense bullet slides aloud; the audience reads ahead and tunes you out.
**Fix:** one idea per slide; you carry the message.

## Cleverness over clarity
Jargon, puns, complex framing to sound smart. **Fix:** plainest accurate words;
concrete over abstract.

## Abstractions without examples
"We drive synergistic value" means nothing. **Fix:** one vivid example per point.

## No signposting
The audience can't tell where they are. **Fix:** preview, transition, recap.

## Not rehearsing aloud
Reading it in your head isn't rehearsal. **Fix:** 3+ timed, out-loud, standing runs.

## Rushing / no pauses
Speeding, trailing off, filler. **Fix:** slow the key line; pause to frame it.

## Winging Q&A / bluffing
Faking answers destroys credibility. **Fix:** prep the worst questions; "I'll
follow up" is fine.

## No call to action
Ending with "any questions?" instead of the ask. **Fix:** end on one clear step.

## Accusation over observation (hard conversations)
"You always…" triggers defense. **Fix:** shared goal + specific observation +
impact + request, then listen.
