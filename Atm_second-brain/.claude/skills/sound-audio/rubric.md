# Sound & Audio Readiness Rubric (binary — every box must hold before delivering)

- [ ] Mic type and polar pattern are matched to the source and room (reject what you don't want, not just capture what you do).
- [ ] Mic distance/placement is set deliberately (proximity effect, off-axis pop control) rather than left at a default.
- [ ] The room is treated or chosen to reduce reflections/noise; capture happens in the deadest practical space.
- [ ] Gain is staged so recorded peaks sit around -12 to -6 dBFS with headroom; nothing clips at 0 dBFS.
- [ ] Recording is monitored on headphones (or was) so noise, clipping, and plosives are caught at the source.
- [ ] Problems were fixed at the source where possible; post-repair is used only for what capture could not prevent.
- [ ] Denoise/cleanup is applied with a light hand (no underwater/artifact-laden voice).
- [ ] Signal chain is in order: clean → EQ → compression → de-ess → limiter (not reordered).
- [ ] EQ is subtractive-first: problem frequencies are cut before any boost, not boosted across the board.
- [ ] Compression is for consistency, not loudness; no audible pumping/breathing.
- [ ] Dialogue/voice is intelligible and sits on top, verified on a phone/laptop speaker (not only studio monitors).
- [ ] Music and SFX are ducked/leveled so they support and do not bury the voice.
- [ ] Audio is in sync with picture, with no drift, and room tone bridges edits.
- [ ] Loudness is metered to the correct platform LUFS target with true peak under the ceiling (-1 dBTP), not set by ear.
- [ ] Mix has been referenced on multiple playback systems and A/B'd against a known reference at matched loudness.
- [ ] Any AI-generated/voice-cloned audio is disclosed and all music/samples are properly licensed.
- [ ] `personal/` recall has been consulted (the user's gear, room, deliverable specs, prior project settings); any conflict with generic advice is flagged and personal/ wins.
