# Leadership & Management Rubric (BINARY)

## Per direct report
- [ ] Has a recurring, protected 1:1 run on *their* agenda, not a status readout.
- [ ] Knows the current top outcome expected of them, stated as a result, not a task list.
- [ ] Has received specific, behavioral feedback within the last two weeks, close to the event.
- [ ] Owns at least one outcome with the decision authority to match it.
- [ ] Can name what "good" looks like for their role and how it is measured.
- [ ] Has a stated growth area and one concrete thing being done about it.

## Per decision
- [ ] The decision is actually made within a reasonable time (no silent drift).
- [ ] The decision, the decider, and the *why* were communicated to everyone affected.
- [ ] Disagree-and-commit was invoked if there was dissent; it is not relitigated.

## Team-level
- [ ] Psychological safety is actively protected: bad news/dissent met with curiosity, and the manager admits their own mistakes.
- [ ] The hiring bar is written down and applied via structured interviews, not gut feel.
- [ ] No active mis-hire is being avoided: underperformance is named, supported with a plan, and acted on.
- [ ] The manager's calendar shows time going to highest-leverage work, not just reactive busywork.
- [ ] Conflict between people is surfaced and addressed directly, not routed around.
- [ ] Goals/OKRs (if used) are few, outcome-shaped, and each report connects their work to one.
- [ ] `personal/` recall has been consulted; any conflict with generic advice is flagged and personal/ wins.
