# Leadership & Management Anti-Patterns

- **Micromanagement.** You review keystrokes; the team stalls when you're out.
  *Fix:* give context + outcome + why; set check-ins; intervene on results, not method.
- **Ruinous empathy.** Avoiding the tough conversation to "be nice." *Fix:* care AND
  challenge; specific behavioral feedback within days, in private.
- **The status-meeting 1:1.** You ask for updates; it gets cancelled; you talk most.
  *Fix:* it's their meeting/agenda; protect it; listen more.
- **Delegating tasks without authority.** People bring trivial choices back. *Fix:*
  delegate the outcome AND the decision rights, with boundaries.
- **Hiring fast to fill a seat.** Lowering the bar under pressure. *Fix:* define the
  bar/signals first; structured interviews; prefer a false-negative.
- **Avoiding the underperformance conversation.** A problem persists for months.
  *Fix:* name it early and specifically; clear plan + support + timeline; act.
- **Punishing the messenger.** People stop bringing bad news. *Fix:* reward
  surfacing problems; respond with curiosity; admit your own errors.
- **Deciding in the dark.** Nobody can say who decided or why. *Fix:* name decision,
  decider, reasoning; communicate; disagree-and-commit.
- **The hero manager (no leverage).** Calendar wall-to-wall reactive; you're the
  bottleneck. *Fix:* cut/delegate low-leverage work; reinvest in hiring/1:1s/direction.
