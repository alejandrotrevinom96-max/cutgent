# Leadership & management

The job is producing a high-output team, not being the smartest IC in the room.
Your output is the output of your team plus the teams you influence (Grove). You
scale through leverage — context, feedback, delegation, good decisions — not by
doing more yourself.

## Personal layer overrides this
Recall the user's `personal/` context: who they manage, team size/seniority,
company stage/culture, the situation, and their own style and past calls.
**Personal context wins.** A 2-person startup and a 40-person org need different
cadences. Say so when you override.

## The method
1. **Hire carefully, slow to hire.** Define the bar and must-have signals before
   sourcing. Prefer a false-negative to a costly mis-hire. Structured interviews;
   references for specifics, not vibes.
2. **Set context and outcomes, not tasks.** Give the goal, the why, constraints,
   and how success is measured — then let them own the how. Micromanagement caps
   output at your bandwidth and erodes ownership.
3. **Regular 1:1s on their agenda.** Recurring, protected, rarely cancelled. It is
   *their* meeting for growth, blockers, and signal — not a status report.
4. **Radical candor: clear + kind, fast.** Care personally AND challenge directly.
   Specific, behavioral feedback close to the event; praise in public, correct in
   private. Withholding hard feedback (ruinous empathy) robs people of growth.
5. **Delegate with ownership + authority.** Hand over outcomes and the decision
   authority to match. State the goal, boundaries, check-ins, and who decides what.
   Delegation without authority is just assigning blame.
6. **Build psychological safety.** People do their best work when they can speak up,
   dissent, admit a mistake, or ask a "dumb" question without fear (Project
   Aristotle's top factor). Model it; reward the messenger.
7. **Make and communicate decisions — with the why.** Decide in reasonable time;
   name the decision, the decider, and the reasoning. Disagree-and-commit once made.
8. **Manage your own energy and calendar.** Your time is the team's scarcest
   leverage. Spend it on hiring, 1:1s, unblocking, direction, key decisions; cut or
   delegate the rest.

## Defaults
- Fewer, better hires over filling a seat fast.
- A struggling report needs more context and clearer outcomes, never more surveillance.
- Never cancel a 1:1 to "save time."
- Give the hard feedback this week, small — not at the annual review, large.
- If you delegate a task, delegate the call that goes with it.
