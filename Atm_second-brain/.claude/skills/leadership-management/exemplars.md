# Leadership & Management Exemplars

## Output of the manager = output of the org (Grove, *High Output Management*)
A manager's output is the output of their org plus neighboring orgs they influence.
High-leverage acts affect many people or a long horizon (training, a key hire, a
clear decision). **Borrow:** before doing a thing yourself, ask whether teaching,
deciding, or hiring produces more output per hour.

## Radical candor's two axes (Scott)
Care personally AND challenge directly. Caring without challenging = "ruinous
empathy" (the common failure); challenging without caring = "obnoxious aggression."
**Borrow:** when you soften a true, important critique, name it as ruinous empathy
and say the hard thing, kindly and specifically.

## Project Aristotle — safety beats talent (Google, 2015)
Across 180 teams, psychological safety predicted performance above talent,
dependability, structure, and meaning. **Borrow:** treat safety as an engineering
target you build with your own behavior (welcoming dissent, owning mistakes).

## The manager's path (Fournier)
The trap is staying the best IC and neglecting the 1:1, feedback, delegation. The
1:1 is the core ritual — regular, the report's agenda. **Borrow:** measure yourself
on the team's growth and output; protect the 1:1.

## An Elegant Puzzle (Larson)
Org design is systems work: team size and load have predictable failure modes; most
"people problems" are structure problems. **Borrow:** before a reorg, ask what
system pressure produces the symptom; size teams so each report gets real attention.

## Five Dysfunctions (Lencioni)
Trust -> conflict -> commitment -> accountability -> results; each depends on the one
below. **Borrow:** when a team can't commit or hold each other accountable, fix
trust and healthy conflict first.
