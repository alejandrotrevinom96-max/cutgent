# Sources

## Canonical (craft & language of the image)
- Blain Brown — *Cinematography: Theory and Practice*. The standard text on
  exposure, lighting, lensing, composition, and workflow.
- Joseph V. Mascelli — *The Five C's of Cinematography*. Camera angles,
  continuity, cutting, close-ups, composition — the grammar most rules trace to.
- Gustavo Mercado — *The Filmmaker's Eye*. Shot-by-shot breakdown of why specific
  compositions and focal lengths produce specific feelings.
- Bruce Block — *The Visual Story*. Visual structure: contrast/affinity in
  space, line, tone, color, movement, and rhythm as story tools.
- Roy Thompson & Christopher Bowen — *Grammar of the Shot* / *Grammar of the
  Edit*. Shot sizes, screen direction, the 180° rule, and how shots cut.
- David A. Ross / Kris Malkiewicz — *Cinematography* (lighting and lab/grade
  fundamentals).

## Practitioners worth studying directly
- Roger Deakins (motivated single-source lighting, negative space; his ASC/podcast
  notes), Emmanuel Lubezki (natural light, long takes), Gordon Willis (shadow and
  underexposure), Gregg Toland (deep focus), Vittorio Storaro (color as meaning),
  Christopher Doyle (frames and movement), Janusz Kamiński, Bradford Young.
- *American Cinematographer* magazine and the ASC for current technique and
  case-by-case lighting/lens breakdowns.

## Note on freshness
Specifics about cameras, sensors, lenses, codecs, log curves, LUTs, lighting
fixtures, and resolution/aspect standards are **point-in-time** — gear and formats
change fast, so re-verify current models, latitude, and workflows before a shoot.
The compositional and lighting *principles* age slowly; the *specs* do not. The
`personal/` layer (the user's own kit, established looks, brand, and project
references, pulled via recall) supersedes everything here when present.
