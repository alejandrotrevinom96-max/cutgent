# Cinematography Exemplars (borrow the mechanism, not the surface)

## 1. *Citizen Kane* — deep focus + low angles (Gregg Toland)
Toland kept foreground and far background simultaneously sharp (deep focus) and
shot Kane from below through built ceilings. **Mechanism:** deep focus forces the
eye to *choose* within a charged frame and binds character to environment; low
angles grant power, then trap it in the room above. **Borrow:** when a scene is
about a character's relationship to their world or their dominance, use deep
depth of field and a low eyeline instead of cutting between separated subjects.

## 2. *Blade Runner 2049* — single motivated source, vast negative space (Roger Deakins)
Deakins lights huge sets off one strong, plausible source (a window, a wall of
fog-lit color) and leaves the human small in oceans of empty frame.
**Mechanism:** one motivated key creates direction, mood, and believability;
negative space says isolation and scale without a line of dialogue. **Borrow:**
resist over-lighting — find the one source the world justifies, then let
emptiness and a single light do the emotional work.

## 3. *Children of Men* — the long take as immersion (Emmanuel Lubezki)
Extended unbroken handheld takes (the car ambush, the battle) refuse the safety
of cutting away. **Mechanism:** continuous time + handheld motion = real-time
dread; the audience can't escape because the camera doesn't. **Borrow:** when you
want unbearable presence rather than coverage, plan a choreographed oner with
blocking and camera move rehearsed together — but only when continuity *is* the
point.

## 4. *The Godfather* — underexposed faces, top light (Gordon Willis)
Willis ("the Prince of Darkness") let Brando's eyes fall into shadow under hard
overhead light. **Mechanism:** withholding the eyes withholds intent; darkness
where the audience expects exposure reads as moral murk and power. **Borrow:**
you can expose *under* the safe level on purpose — let shadow conceal to build
menace, instead of filling every face evenly.

## 5. *Mad Max: Fury Road* — center-framing + cut on the same point (John Seale / Margaret Sixel)
Action is composed dead-center and cut so the eye never hunts between shots, even
at extreme speed. **Mechanism:** centering the subject of interest keeps the
focal point fixed across rapid cuts, so chaos stays legible. **Borrow:** for fast
action, break the thirds rule on purpose — keep the point of interest centered
and consistent so cuts read instantly.

## 6. *In the Mood for Love* — frames-within-frames + long-lens compression (Christopher Doyle / Mark Lee Ping-bing)
Characters are shot through doorways, mirrors, and corridors on long lenses that
flatten and crowd the space. **Mechanism:** the frame-within-a-frame and
compression make the lovers feel watched, constrained, and pressed together by
circumstance. **Borrow:** to show repression or surveillance, stage the subject
behind a foreground aperture and reach for a longer lens to compress them into it.
