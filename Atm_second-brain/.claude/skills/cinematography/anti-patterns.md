# Cinematography Anti-Patterns (detectable failure modes + fix)

## 1. No intent — pretty but empty
**Tell:** you can describe the shot's look but not its emotional job or where the
eye should go.
**Fix:** state the one beat and the one subject before touching the camera. If you
can't, you're decorating, not shooting. Let intent pick the tools.

## 2. Accidental dead-center / wrong negative space
**Tell:** subject parked in the middle by default, headroom too large, no
lead/look room so the figure stares or walks into the edge.
**Fix:** compose on a third (or center *on purpose* for symmetry/action), fix
headroom, and leave space ahead of the gaze or motion.

## 3. Lens chosen by convenience, not psychology
**Tell:** "I just zoomed to frame it" — a wide used at portrait distance (face
distortion) or a long lens flattening a scene that needed space.
**Fix:** pick focal length for feeling, then move the camera to frame. Wide for
immersion/space, long for compression/isolation, normal for neutral.

## 4. Flat, frontal, unmotivated lighting
**Tell:** even soft light from camera, no shadow side, subject and background at
the same brightness, no source you can point to in the room.
**Fix:** establish a key direction and quality, set a key:fill ratio for mood,
separate the subject from the background, and motivate the source.

## 5. Blown highlights / muddy shadows from lazy exposure
**Tell:** clipped windows or skies, or crushed blacks with detail you needed,
because exposure was "auto" or eyeballed.
**Fix:** expose for the subject while protecting the highlights you must keep;
know the camera's latitude; set contrast intent deliberately and shoot log only
if you'll grade.

## 6. Restless camera — movement without meaning
**Tell:** constant slow drift, push-ins on nothing, or handheld jitter on a calm
scene; the move doesn't change what the shot means.
**Fix:** move only to intensify, reveal, follow, or unsettle on purpose.
Otherwise lock it off — stillness is a choice too.

## 7. Crossing the line / broken eyelines
**Tell:** in the edit, two people in a conversation suddenly look the same
direction or appear to swap sides; screen direction flips.
**Fix:** pick a line of action, keep the camera on one side (180° rule), match
eyelines in singles, and only cross the line on-camera or by intent.

## 8. Coverage that won't cut
**Tell:** adjacent shots are nearly the same size/angle (jump cuts), no
establishing or insert, or a static frame with no exit so the editor is stuck.
**Fix:** vary shot size and angle by ~30°/one size between cuts, get an
establisher and inserts, and leave each shot somewhere to cut.
