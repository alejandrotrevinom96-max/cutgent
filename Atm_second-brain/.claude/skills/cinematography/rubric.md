# Cinematography Shot Rubric (binary — every box must be checked before calling a shot done)

- [ ] The shot's single emotional beat and the one subject the eye must land on are stated.
- [ ] Focal length is chosen for its psychological effect (wide/normal/long), not just to fit the frame.
- [ ] Subject sits on a deliberate composition (thirds or intentional symmetry), not accidental dead-center.
- [ ] Headroom is correct and there is lead/look room in the direction of gaze or motion.
- [ ] Depth is built with at least one device (foreground layer, leading line, or frame-within-frame) where appropriate.
- [ ] Aperture/depth-of-field is a deliberate choice (isolate vs. tie to environment), not a reflex.
- [ ] Exposure protects the highlights/subject the shot must keep, and contrast intent is set on purpose.
- [ ] Key light direction and quality (hard/soft) are chosen, and the key:fill ratio matches the intended mood.
- [ ] Every light is motivated by a plausible in-world source (or the unmotivated choice is intentional).
- [ ] Subject is separated from the background (via back/rim light, depth, contrast, or color).
- [ ] Camera movement changes meaning (push/pull/track/handheld for a reason); otherwise it is locked off.
- [ ] Actors are blocked first and staging supports the composition over the duration of the shot.
- [ ] The 180° line is chosen and held (or crossed on-camera/with intent); eyelines and screen direction are consistent.
- [ ] Coverage cuts: shot sizes/angles vary enough from adjacent shots to avoid jump cuts, and the shot leaves room to cut.
- [ ] Palette and contrast are consistent with the scene's color intent and encode story, not decoration.
- [ ] `personal/` recall has been consulted; any conflict with generic craft is flagged and personal/ wins.
