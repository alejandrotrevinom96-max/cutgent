# Exemplars — study these and WHY

1. **Pixar shorts / "Luxo Jr." & the 12 principles in practice.** A desk lamp reads
   as a character purely through spacing, anticipation, and follow-through. Timing,
   weight, and appeal — not render quality — make motion feel alive.

2. **Disney's Nine Old Men — *The Illusion of Life* reference clips.** The source of
   the 12 principles. Squash/stretch and overlapping action; medium-independent.

3. **Ian Hubert — "Lazy Tutorials" / set dressing.** A few photo-textured kitbash
   pieces, smart framing, motivated light sell a whole environment fast. Spend craft
   where the camera looks; fake the rest — the right model for where generative
   assets belong (dressing, not hero deformation).

4. **Blender Open Movies ("Spring," "Sprite Fright," "Charge").** Full open
   pipelines with .blend files — real edge-flow, weight painting, lighting in
   shipped work.

5. **ILM / Weta FX shot breakdowns.** Top-tier integration — matched lighting/HDRI,
   plausible PBR, motion respecting real weight. See how layers (layout → anim →
   sim → light → comp) build a believable frame.

6. **A great demo reel (Animation Mentor / Gnomon grad reels).** Calibrates the bar
   for clean blocking-to-polish performance — clear arcs, anticipation, weight; weak
   shots float or move uniformly.
