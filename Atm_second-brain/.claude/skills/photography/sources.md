# Sources

## Canonical (craft & method)
- Ansel Adams — *The Camera*, *The Negative*, *The Print* (the trilogy). Exposure
  as deliberate tonal placement; the Zone System; previsualization.
- Henri Cartier-Bresson — *The Decisive Moment*. Timing, geometry, and gesture in
  candid/street work.
- Michael Freeman — *The Photographer's Eye* and *The Photographer's Mind*.
  Composition, design, and the intent behind the frame.
- Bryan Peterson — *Understanding Exposure*. The exposure triangle and creative
  trade-offs in plain terms.
- Joe McNally — *The Moment It Clicks* / *The Hot Shoe Diaries*. Practical light
  shaping with small flash.
- Light Science & Magic (Hunter, Biver, Fuqua, Reid). The physics of how surfaces
  reflect light — the most rigorous lighting reference.
- Scott Kelby — *The Digital Photography Book* / Lightroom & Photoshop books.
  Practical, current digital edit workflow.

## Photographers to study (taste calibration)
- Steve McCurry, Annie Leibovitz, Sebastião Salgado, Richard Avedon, Irving Penn,
  Vivian Maier, Sally Mann, Gregory Crewdson, Saul Leiter (color/street).

## References & standards
- Histogram and expose-to-the-right (ETTR) practice.
- NPPA / Reuters / AP photo-manipulation guidelines for documentary honesty;
  model and property release norms for commercial use.

## Note on freshness
Gear specifics — sensor ISO performance, lens lineups, body capabilities, and
software UI/feature names — are point-in-time and change with every release; treat
any gear claim as date-stamped and re-verify before relying on it. Craft (light,
composition, moment, the edit order) is durable. The `personal/` layer (the user's
own bodies, lenses, presets, signature look, and recurring subjects) supersedes
everything here when present.
