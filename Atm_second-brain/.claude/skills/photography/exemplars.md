# Photography Exemplars (borrow the mechanism, not the surface)

## 1. Henri Cartier-Bresson — the decisive moment (street/documentary)
"Behind the Gare Saint-Lazare" (man leaping a puddle) works because geometry,
gesture, and timing lock into one instant — a fraction earlier or later and the
frame collapses. **Mechanism:** pre-compose the stage (the lines, the reflection),
then wait and release at peak gesture. **Borrow:** set your frame first, anticipate
the moment, fire once at the apex instead of spraying and hoping.

## 2. Steve McCurry — "Afghan Girl" (environmental portrait)
The power is soft directional window-quality light, piercing eye contact with a
sharp catchlight, complementary color (green eyes/shawl against muted skin), and
ruthless simplicity — nothing in the frame competes with the face. **Mechanism:**
flattering soft side light + color relationship + a clean background concentrate
all attention on expression. **Borrow:** isolate the subject, control the color
palette, nail focus on the eyes, light the face softly.

## 3. Ansel Adams — Zone System & expose-for-tone (landscape)
"Moonrise, Hernandez" depends on previsualizing the final print and exposing/
developing so highlights and shadows land where he wanted them. **Mechanism:**
treat exposure as deliberate tonal placement, not auto-brightness; protect the
highlights and build the shadow detail you'll need. **Borrow:** expose to the
right, read the histogram, and edit toward a tonal intent you decided in advance.

## 4. Annie Leibovitz — shaped light & directed portraiture (editorial)
Her portraits read as constructed scenes: large modified key light for soft
modeling, intentional ratio for mood, and direction that gives the subject an
action or attitude rather than a stiff smile. **Mechanism:** build the light to
sculpt the face, then direct a moment within it. **Borrow:** choose a key light
size/position on purpose, set fill to taste, and direct (give a verb), don't just
pose.

## 5. Sebastião Salgado — tonal drama & dignity (photojournalism)
His black-and-white work uses dramatic side/back light, deep tonal range, and
strong compositional lines to give scale and gravity while preserving the
subject's dignity — and stays within documentary honesty. **Mechanism:** hard
directional light + composed layers + restrained processing create epic tone
without fabricating content. **Borrow:** use light direction and composition for
drama instead of HDR tricks; keep the edit honest in reportage.

## 6. Gregory Crewdson / Joe McNally — light as the whole subject (constructed)
Crewdson's cinematic tableaux (and McNally's flash work) treat light placement as
the primary creative act, building mood from many controlled sources. **Mechanism:**
each light has a job (key, fill, rim, background, practicals) and the composition
is engineered around them. **Borrow:** when you can control light, assign every
source a role and previsualize the lit result before shooting.
